A small group of mathematicians and engineers dared to ask whether machines could truly think. Alan Turing proposed a test. John McCarthy coined the term "artificial intelligence". The first programs played chess, proved theorems, and learned. The field was born — wildly optimistic, chronically underfunded, and absolutely certain it would succeed.
