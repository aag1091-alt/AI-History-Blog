From Ramon Llull's logic machines to Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine, thinkers imagined devices that could reason. Philosophers and inventors laid the conceptual groundwork for computation, asking whether thought itself could be mechanized. These early visions planted seeds that would take centuries to germinate.
