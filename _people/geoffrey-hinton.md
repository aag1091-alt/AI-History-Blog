Geoffrey Hinton, born on December 6, 1947, is a British-Canadian computer scientist and cognitive psychologist renowned for his pioneering work in artificial neural networks. His contributions to the field, including the popularization of backpropagation, have profoundly influenced the development of modern AI. After a decade at Google Brain, Hinton's departure in 2023 highlighted his growing concerns about AI's societal risks.
