Geoffrey Hinton (born 6 December 1947) is a British-Canadian computer scientist and cognitive psychologist whose decades of research on artificial neural networks earned him the title 'the Godfather of AI' and a Nobel Prize. As University Professor Emeritus at the University of Toronto, he divided his time between academia and Google Brain from 2013 to 2023, before departing Google to speak freely about the risks of AI technology.
