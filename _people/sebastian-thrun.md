Sebastian Thrun, born in 1967, is a German-American computer scientist and entrepreneur renowned for his pioneering work in autonomous vehicles and robotics. At Google, he co-founded Google X and led the development of Stanley, a self-driving car that triumphed in the 2005 DARPA Grand Challenge. Additionally, he established Udacity, an innovative education platform dedicated to imparting practical skills in fields like artificial intelligence and robotics.
