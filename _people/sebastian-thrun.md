Sebastian Thrun is a German-American computer scientist and entrepreneur whose work on autonomous vehicles and AI has reshaped both transportation and education. His Stanford team built Stanley, which won the 2005 DARPA Grand Challenge and earned a place in the Smithsonian, before he moved to Google to co-found Google X and its self-driving car program. He later co-founded Udacity, bringing cutting-edge tech education to millions online.
