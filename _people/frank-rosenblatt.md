Frank Rosenblatt was a Cornell psychologist and computer scientist who electrified the world in 1958 by unveiling the Perceptron—a machine that could actually learn from experience, igniting visions of truly intelligent computers. His work was sidelined after Minsky and Papert's 1969 critique exposed the limits of single-layer networks, but the core ideas he championed roared back to life in the deep learning revolution. Rosenblatt died in a sailing accident in 1971 at just 43, never witnessing the vindication of the neural network paradigm he helped create.
