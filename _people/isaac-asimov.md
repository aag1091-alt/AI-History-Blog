Isaac Asimov (c. January 2, 1920 – April 6, 1992) was an American writer and professor of biochemistry at Boston University whose staggering output of over 500 books made him one of the most prolific authors in history. Regarded as one of the 'Big Three' science fiction writers alongside Robert A. Heinlein and Arthur C. Clarke, he created the Foundation series—a grand reimagining of the rise and fall of civilizations in deep space—and the Robot series, whose Three Laws of Robotics became a foundational framework for real-world AI ethics discussions.
