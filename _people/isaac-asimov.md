Isaac Asimov (c. 1920–1992) was an American writer and professor of biochemistry at Boston University who became one of the 'Big Three' science fiction writers alongside Robert A. Heinlein and Arthur C. Clarke. His Foundation series reimagined the rise and fall of civilizations on a galactic scale, while his Robot series gave the world the Three Laws of Robotics—ideas that continue to influence AI research and ethics decades after his death. A staggeringly prolific author of more than 500 books, Asimov left his mark on nearly every corner of literature, from hard science fiction to guides on the Bible and Shakespeare.
