John Searle (1932–2025) was an American philosopher who became one of AI's most famous skeptics with his 1980 Chinese Room argument, which challenged the notion that a computer running a program could ever possess genuine understanding. A towering figure at UC Berkeley from 1959, he made landmark contributions to the philosophy of language through speech act theory and to the philosophy of mind through his theory of biological naturalism, insisting that consciousness arises from specific biological processes no algorithm can replicate.
