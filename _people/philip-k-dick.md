Philip K. Dick (1928–1982) was an American science fiction writer whose restless imagination produced 45 novels and some 121 short stories exploring the fractures between reality and illusion, human and artificial, free will and control. Born in Chicago and raised in the San Francisco Bay Area, he began publishing at 23 and spent three decades spinning out worlds haunted by monopolistic corporations, authoritarian governments, and altered states of consciousness—work that would profoundly shape both literature and film long after his death.
