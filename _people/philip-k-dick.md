Philip K. Dick, an American science fiction author born in Chicago in 1928, explored the depths of reality, perception, and identity in a prolific career marked by philosophical depth and visionary storytelling. Moving to the San Francisco Bay Area early in life, he began publishing at 23, with his work often delving into dystopian themes and alternative realities, making him one of the most influential figures in 20th-century science fiction.
