Born on 17 January 1963, Jürgen Schmidhuber is a German computer scientist widely described as a leading pioneer of modern artificial intelligence. He is best known for his foundational work on LSTM networks, which revolutionized natural language processing and sequence modeling across research and industry. As scientific director at the Dalle Molle Institute and professor at KAUST, he continues to push the boundaries of artificial general intelligence and the theoretical foundations of curiosity and creativity.
