Murray Campbell is a Canadian computer scientist who helped change the world's understanding of what machines could achieve. As a core member of the IBM team behind Deep Blue, he engineered the system that defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov in 1997—a landmark moment in AI history. His work on search algorithms and massively parallel game-tree evaluation laid groundwork for modern AI systems tackling complex decision-making.
