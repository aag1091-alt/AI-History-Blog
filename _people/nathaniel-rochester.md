Nathaniel Rochester (1919–2001) was an American computer scientist at IBM who helped architect the machine that proved computers could think — and then helped define what 'thinking' meant. As designer of the IBM 701 and co-organizer of the 1956 Dartmouth Conference, he stood at the crossroads of hardware and nascent artificial intelligence, bridging the gap between building powerful machines and imagining what they might one day become.
