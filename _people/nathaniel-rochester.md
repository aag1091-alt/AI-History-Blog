Nathaniel Rochester, born in 1752 and passing away in 1831, was a Revolutionary War veteran and a key figure in early American land speculation. He is best known for founding the city of Rochester, New York, originally named Rochesterville. His legacy includes a rich collection of personal papers that offer insights into the early history of the settlement and his contributions as a civic leader.
