Nathaniel Rochester, born in 1752, was an American Revolutionary War soldier and land speculator. He is best known for founding the city of Rochester, New York, which grew into a significant cultural and industrial center. His life's work and legacy are preserved through historical documents, offering a rich narrative of early American settlement and development.
