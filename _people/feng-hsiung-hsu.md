Feng-hsiung Hsu is a renowned computer scientist celebrated for his pioneering work in chess computer technology. He gained prominence for developing Deep Thought, which became the first computer to challenge a reigning world chess champion. Hsu's subsequent work culminated in Deep Blue, the IBM supercomputer that famously defeated world champion Garry Kasparov in 1997, marking a significant milestone in the field of artificial intelligence.
