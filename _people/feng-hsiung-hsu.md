Feng-hsiung Hsu, born January 1, 1959, is a Taiwanese-American computer scientist and electrical engineer renowned for his pivotal role in developing chess-playing computers. As the architect of Deep Thought and principal designer of IBM Deep Blue, Hsu significantly advanced the field of artificial intelligence, notably by creating the first chess computer to defeat grandmasters and achieve a certified grandmaster rating. His contributions have been recognized with the prestigious ACM Grace Murray Hopper Award.
