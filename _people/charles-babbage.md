Charles Babbage (1791–1871) was an English polymath—mathematician, philosopher, inventor, and mechanical engineer—who originated the concept of a digital programmable computer. His Difference Engine was the first mechanical computer, and his more ambitious Analytical Engine anticipated virtually every key principle of modern computing, earning him the title 'father of the computer.' Beyond computing, he was a prominent London intellectual whose 1832 book Economy of Manufactures and Machinery influenced both science and industry.
