Charles Babbage (1791–1871) was an English polymath renowned for his visionary work in mathematics, philosophy, and engineering. As the conceptual father of the computer, Babbage's inventions, including the difference engine and the analytical engine, revolutionized the trajectory of computing technology. His extensive influence extended beyond technical innovation to the social and economic dynamics of Victorian London.
