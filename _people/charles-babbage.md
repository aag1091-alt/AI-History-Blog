Charles Babbage (1791–1871) was a British mathematician and inventor whose obsession with eliminating human error from mathematical tables led him to envision machines that could think. His Analytical Engine — complete with a mill (CPU), store (memory), and punch-card input — outlined the blueprint for every computer that followed, even though it was never fully built in his lifetime. Equal parts visionary and cantankerous perfectionist, Babbage spent decades and a fortune chasing a future the world wasn't ready to build.
