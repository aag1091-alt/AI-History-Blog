Mary Shelley, an English novelist and writer, is best known for her seminal work Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus, which is an early example of science fiction. Born to political philosopher William Godwin and women's rights advocate Mary Wollstonecraft, Shelley was raised with a strong intellectual foundation that influenced her own literary pursuits and advocacy.
