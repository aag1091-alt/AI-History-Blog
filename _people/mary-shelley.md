Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley, born in 1797, was an English novelist known for writing Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus, a seminal work that explores the ethical and existential implications of scientific progress. Orphaned of her mother shortly after birth, Shelley was educated by her father, political philosopher William Godwin, and later became involved with the Romantic poet Percy Bysshe Shelley, whom she married. Her literary achievements include not only her own novels but also her role in preserving and promoting the works of her husband.
