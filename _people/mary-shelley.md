Mary Shelley, born Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin, was an English novelist and essayist, best known for her Gothic novel 'Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus'. Her life was marked by early tragedy and intellectual rigor, as she was educated by her father, the political philosopher William Godwin, and her mother, the feminist Mary Wollstonecraft, who died shortly after her birth. Shelley's literary career included not only her own works but also the preservation and promotion of her husband Percy Bysshe Shelley's poetry and philosophy.
