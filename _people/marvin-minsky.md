Marvin Minsky (1927–2016) was a Harvard- and Princeton-trained American mathematician and cognitive scientist who co-founded MIT's AI laboratory and spent over five decades shaping the field of artificial intelligence. Widely regarded as one of the 'fathers of AI,' he received the 1969 Turing Award and held the Toshiba Professorship of Media Arts & Sciences at MIT. His influential book 'The Society of Mind' proposed that human intelligence arises not from a single mechanism but from the interplay of countless simpler processes.
