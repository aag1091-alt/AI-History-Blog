Kenton Lee is a natural language processing researcher at Google who has shaped how machines understand references and retrieve knowledge from text. His work on neural coreference resolution and retrieval-augmented language models helped push NLP systems toward more flexible, end-to-end learning, reducing reliance on brittle hand-crafted pipelines.
