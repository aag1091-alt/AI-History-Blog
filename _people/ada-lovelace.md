Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace (1815–1852), was an English mathematician who saw the future of computing before computers existed. Working with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine, she wrote what is considered the first computer program and, crucially, recognized the machine's potential far beyond mere number-crunching. The only legitimate child of poet Lord Byron, she channeled literary imagination into mathematical rigor, earning her place as a founding visionary of computer science.
