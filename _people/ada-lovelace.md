Ada Lovelace, born Augusta Ada Byron, was an English mathematician and writer renowned for her work on Charles Babbage's analytical engine. Despite being a child of the poet Lord Byron, Ada was raised by her mother, who instilled a strong interest in mathematics. Her visionary insights about the analytical engine's capacity to manipulate symbols in addition to numbers earned her recognition as the world's first computer programmer.
