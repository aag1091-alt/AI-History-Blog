Ada Lovelace, born Augusta Ada Byron, was an English mathematician and writer who became renowned for her work on Charles Babbage's analytical engine. Despite her early life struggles, including the separation of her parents and her own health issues, Lovelace pursued her passion for mathematics and science. Her notes on the analytical engine, particularly her algorithm for calculating Bernoulli numbers, are celebrated as the world's first computer program, cementing her legacy as a pioneer in the field of computing.
