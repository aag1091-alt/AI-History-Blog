Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, was a Victorian-era mathematician who saw the future of computing a century before it arrived. Working alongside Charles Babbage, she translated and annotated a paper on his Analytical Engine, appending a set of notes that contained what is now regarded as the first computer program. Her visionary insight that machines could process not just numbers but any form of symbolic information earned her lasting recognition as a prophet of the digital age.
