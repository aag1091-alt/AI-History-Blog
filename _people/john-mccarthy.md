John McCarthy (1927–2011) was an American computer scientist and cognitive scientist who gave artificial intelligence its name and its founding moment—the 1956 Dartmouth workshop. He created Lisp, one of the oldest high-level programming languages still in use, and spent decades at Stanford advancing formal reasoning about common sense in machines.
