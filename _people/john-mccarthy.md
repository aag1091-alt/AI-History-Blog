John McCarthy, an American computer scientist and cognitive scientist (1927-2011), is celebrated as a key figure in the development of artificial intelligence. He is best known for coining the term 'Artificial Intelligence' and for his seminal work on the Dartmouth Conference in 1955, which outlined the initial goals and methodologies of AI research.
