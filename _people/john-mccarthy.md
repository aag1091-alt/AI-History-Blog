John McCarthy (1927-2011) was an American computer scientist and cognitive scientist, renowned as a pioneer of Artificial Intelligence. He coined the term 'AI' and organized the seminal 1956 Dartmouth Conference, which marked the birth of AI as a field of study. McCarthy's invention of the Lisp programming language revolutionized the field, providing a powerful tool for AI researchers.
