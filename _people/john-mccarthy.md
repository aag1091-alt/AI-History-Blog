John McCarthy (1927–2011) gave artificial intelligence its name and then spent a lifetime building its foundations. A Stanford professor and Turing Award winner, he created Lisp, championed mathematical logic as the backbone of machine reasoning, and envisioned a future where computers would rival human intellect—decades before the rest of the world caught on.
