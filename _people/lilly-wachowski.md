Lilly Wachowski, born December 29, 1967, is an American film director and writer who, along with her sister Lana, has made significant contributions to science fiction cinema. Known collectively as the Wachowskis, they made their mark with Bound (1996) and reached global recognition with The Matrix, an influential film that redefined the action genre. Their work often explores complex narratives and innovative visual styles, solidifying their status as influential filmmakers.
