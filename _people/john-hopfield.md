John Hopfield is an American physicist whose 1982 invention of the associative neural network — now called the Hopfield network — pulled artificial intelligence out of its dormant winter and reignited large-scale interest in the field. An emeritus professor at Princeton University, he has spent decades applying the lens of physics to the brain, exploring how networks of neurons store and retrieve memories. His work earned him the 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics, shared with Geoffrey Hinton, for foundational contributions that enable modern machine learning.
