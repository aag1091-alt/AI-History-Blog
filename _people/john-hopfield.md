John Hopfield, born in 1933, is an American physicist and emeritus professor at Princeton University, renowned for his groundbreaking work in neural networks and AI. His invention of the Hopfield network in 1982 reinvigorated AI research during a period of stagnation, and his contributions continue to influence the fields of physics and neuroscience.
