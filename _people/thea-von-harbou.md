Thea von Harbou (1888–1954) was a German screenwriter and novelist whose imaginative storytelling helped shape science fiction cinema. She is best remembered for writing both the novel and screenplay for Metropolis (1927), a film whose vision of a mechanized future city remains iconic nearly a century later. As the creative partner and wife of director Fritz Lang, she co-authored some of the most visually ambitious films of the silent and early sound era.
