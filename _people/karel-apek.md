Karel Čapek was a Czech playwright and novelist who permanently shaped how humanity thinks about artificial beings by giving the world the word 'robot' in his 1920 play R.U.R. His work blended science fiction with sharp political satire, exploring themes of dehumanization, mass production, and the ethical limits of technological ambition. He died in 1938 on the eve of the Nazi occupation he had spent years warning against.
