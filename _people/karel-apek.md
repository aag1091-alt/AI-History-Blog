Czech writer, playwright, critic, and journalist Karel Čapek (1890–1938) is renowned for his influential science fiction works, particularly 'R.U.R.' and 'War with the Newts,' which explore the ethical dimensions of technological advancement and political corruption. An advocate for democratic values and free speech, Čapek's works often reflected the social and political turmoil of his era.
