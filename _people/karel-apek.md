Czech writer Karel Čapek (1890-1938) is celebrated for his pioneering science fiction and political satire. Best known for introducing the term 'robot' in his play R.U.R., Čapek's work often critiqued the social and political upheavals of his era. His writings, including 'War with the Newts,' reflect a deep concern for human rights and democracy, earning him seven nominations for the Nobel Prize in Literature.
