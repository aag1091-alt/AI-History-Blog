James Cameron is a Canadian filmmaker and deep-sea explorer born on August 16, 1954, whose films have grossed over $10 billion worldwide, making him the second-highest-grossing film director of all time. From The Terminator to Titanic to Avatar, he has consistently pushed the boundaries of what's possible in cinema, blending cutting-edge technology with powerful storytelling. A recipient of three Academy Awards, two Primetime Emmy Awards, and four Golden Globe Awards, Cameron remains one of the most influential figures in the post-New Hollywood era.
