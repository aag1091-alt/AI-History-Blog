Samuel Butler (1835–1902) was an influential English author and critic best known for his satirical novel 'Erewhon', a world-renowned literary work that critiques Victorian society and technology. Additionally, he wrote 'The Way of All Flesh', a semi-autobiographical novel that challenged prevailing religious and moral conventions.
