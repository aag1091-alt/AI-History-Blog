Samuel Butler was a Victorian-era author and iconoclast who, in the 1860s, became one of the first thinkers to seriously argue that machines could evolve intelligence and eventually surpass their human creators. His essay 'Darwin among the Machines' and his novel 'Erewhon' laid out a chillingly prescient vision of technological evolution that anticipates modern debates about AI alignment and existential risk. Writing over a century before the term 'artificial intelligence' was coined, Butler transformed Darwin's theory of natural selection into a warning about humanity's mechanical offspring.
