Sepp Hochreiter is a German computer scientist whose co-invention of the LSTM architecture fundamentally reshaped how machines process sequences, powering breakthroughs in speech recognition, machine translation, and beyond. Based at Johannes Kepler University of Linz, where he heads the Institute for Machine Learning and the LIT AI Lab, he has continued pushing boundaries in meta-learning, reinforcement learning, and bioinformatics. His work bridges deep theoretical insight with practical impact, earning him a central place in the history of modern AI.
