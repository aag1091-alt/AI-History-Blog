Garry Kasparov dominated world chess for two decades, but his greatest legacy may be what happened when he sat across from a machine. His 1997 loss to IBM's Deep Blue became a defining moment in AI history, demonstrating that brute-force computation could overcome human intuition in complex strategy. Rather than retreat, Kasparov became one of AI's most thoughtful public advocates, championing human-machine collaboration and warning that the real danger lies not in intelligent machines but in failing to use them wisely.
