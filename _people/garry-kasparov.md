Garry Kasparov, born Garik Kimovich Weinstein in 1963, is a Russian chess grandmaster, political activist, and writer who reigned as World Chess Champion from 1985 to 2000. He is renowned for his unmatched chess prowess, holding the world's top ranking for over two decades and setting a peak FIDE rating that stood for over a decade. Kasparov's historic match against IBM's Deep Blue in 1997 marked a pivotal moment in the relationship between chess and artificial intelligence.
