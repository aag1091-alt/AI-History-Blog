Garry Kasparov, born in 1963, dominated chess for two decades as World Champion from 1985 to 2000, defending his title against Anatoly Karpov three times. His landmark matches against IBM's Deep Blue in 1996–97 became a defining moment in the history of artificial intelligence, forcing the world to reckon with what machines could achieve. After retiring from competitive chess in 2005, he became a political activist and author, penning works including 'Garry Kasparov on Garry Kasparov.'
