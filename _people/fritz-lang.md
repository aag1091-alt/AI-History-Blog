Fritz Lang, an Austrian film director, screenwriter, and producer, was a pivotal figure in the transition from silent to sound films and from German Expressionism to Hollywood cinema. His work, which includes groundbreaking films like Metropolis and M, established him as a master of cinematic darkness and influenced generations of filmmakers.
