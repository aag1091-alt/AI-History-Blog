Fritz Lang was an Austrian-American filmmaker whose 1927 masterpiece Metropolis gave the world its most enduring image of artificial life—the Maschinenmensch, a gleaming robot built to deceive and control. Decades before AI became a scientific discipline, Lang forced audiences to confront questions about machine autonomy, class warfare driven by automation, and the thin line between creator and creation. His visual language for depicting intelligent machines remains embedded in popular culture, from C-3PO to Ex Machina.
