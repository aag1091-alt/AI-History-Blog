Fritz Lang (1890–1976) was an Austrian filmmaker whose visionary work spanning five decades — from German Expressionist silent films to Hollywood — earned him recognition as one of the most influential filmmakers of all time. Dubbed the 'Master of Darkness' by the British Film Institute, Lang shaped the language of cinema with iconic works like Metropolis and M, blending striking visual style with provocative social commentary.
