David Everett Rumelhart, an American psychologist (1942-2011), was a pioneering figure in the field of cognitive science, particularly known for his work on parallel distributed processing and backpropagation learning algorithms. His research significantly advanced the understanding of human cognition and the computational models that simulate it, laying the groundwork for modern neural network architectures.
