Alex Krizhevsky is a Canadian computer scientist who ignited the deep learning revolution. In 2012, working under Geoffrey Hinton at the University of Toronto alongside Ilya Sutskever, he built AlexNet—a powerful visual-recognition neural network trained on just two GeForce GPU cards—shattering previous benchmarks and proving that GPUs, not CPUs, were the key to unlocking advanced AI. That single breakthrough opened the floodgates for the massive GPU-driven AI models that define the field today.
