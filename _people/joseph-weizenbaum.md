Joseph Weizenbaum (1923-2008) was a pioneering computer scientist and professor at MIT, known for his work in artificial intelligence and the ethics of computing. He developed ELIZA, a groundbreaking program that simulated human-like conversation, which also sparked debates on AI's limitations and societal implications.
