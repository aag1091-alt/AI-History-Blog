Joseph Weizenbaum (1923–2008) was a German-American computer scientist and MIT professor who built ELIZA, the pioneering chatbot that stunned the world by convincingly mimicking human conversation — then spent the rest of his career arguing that machines should never replace human judgment. His influential book 'Computer Power and Human Reason' challenged the AI community to confront the moral responsibilities of their work, making him both a founding figure and one of the sharpest critics of the field he helped create.
