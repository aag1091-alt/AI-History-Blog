Born June 21, 1965, Lana Wachowski is a Polish-American film director, writer, and producer who, alongside her sister Lilly, redefined science fiction cinema with The Matrix trilogy. The Wachowskis' bold storytelling and pioneering visual effects — including the iconic 'bullet time' technique — made The Matrix a cultural touchstone that shaped how audiences and technologists alike think about simulated reality and artificial intelligence.
