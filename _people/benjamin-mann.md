Benjamin Elijah Mays was an American Baptist minister and civil rights leader born in the Jim Crow South to freed sharecroppers. He became a pivotal figure in the civil rights movement by teaching and mentoring influential leaders and advocating for nonviolent resistance and Black self-determination.
