Alan Turing was an English mathematician and computer scientist whose groundbreaking work laid the foundation for modern computing. He introduced the concept of the Turing machine, a theoretical device that can compute anything that is computable, and played a crucial role in cracking Nazi Germany's Enigma code during World War II.
