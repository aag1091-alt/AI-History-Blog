Claude Elwood Shannon (1916–2001) was an American mathematician, electrical engineer, and cryptographer who single-handedly created information theory, transforming how we understand communication and computation. Graduating from the University of Michigan in 1936 with dual degrees in electrical engineering and mathematics, he went on to demonstrate that Boolean algebra could underpin all digital circuits—an insight essential to every computer built since. Hailed by roboticist Rodney Brooks as the 20th-century engineer who contributed the most to 21st-century technologies, Shannon's work remains the bedrock of the digital world.
