Claude Elwood Shannon, an American polymath who graduated from the University of Michigan with dual degrees in mathematics and electrical engineering in 1936, became a pivotal figure in the 20th century for his foundational work in information theory and digital circuit design. His groundbreaking paper 'A Mathematical Theory of Communication' established the field of information theory, influencing modern computing, cryptography, and telecommunications.
