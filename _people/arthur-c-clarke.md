Arthur C. Clarke was a British-Sri Lankan science fiction writer and futurist whose imaginative works shaped how humanity thinks about intelligent machines. His creation of HAL 9000 in 2001: A Space Odyssey became the defining cultural touchstone for AI—a sentient computer that could reason, speak, and ultimately rebel against its creators. A prolific inventor of ideas ahead of their time, Clarke bridged hard science and speculative fiction like few others, earning him a place among the most influential voices in the history of thinking about artificial intelligence.
