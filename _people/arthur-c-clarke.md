Sir Arthur C. Clarke (1917–2008) was a British science fiction writer, futurist, and inventor whose visionary works bridged the gap between scientific imagination and reality. Best known for co-writing 2001: A Space Odyssey and for proposing geostationary satellite orbits, he earned the moniker 'Prophet of the Space Age' and the UNESCO Kalinga Prize for popularising science. A tireless explorer of both outer space and the deep sea, Clarke's influence spans literature, film, and technology.
