# Frankenstein: The First AI Nightmare

The year 1818 saw the birth of a literary masterpiece that would forever shape the way we think about artificial life and the dangers of unchecked ambition: Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein."

**What happened:** In 1818, Mary Shelley, at the young age of 20, anonymously published "Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus." The novel tells the story of Victor Frankenstein, a scientist who creates a sapient creature from body parts in a misguided attempt to play God. Shelley's inspiration for the novel came from her travels through Europe, particularly her visit to the region near Frankenstein Castle in Germany, and her interest in galvanism and alchemy. [Wikipedia — Frankenstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frankenstein)

**Why it matters:** "Frankenstein" is a seminal work in the history of artificial life narratives, serving as a cautionary tale about the perils of unchecked scientific ambition. Shelley's novel introduced the enduring trope of the creator who loses control over their creation, a theme that resonates deeply in contemporary discussions about artificial intelligence. The story's exploration of ethical boundaries and the consequences of playing God continues to influence modern debates on AI ethics and safety.
