**The Birth of AI's Dark Side**

In 1818, Mary Shelley penned a Gothic novel that would forever alter the public's perception of artificial intelligence - Frankenstein.

**What happened:** [Frankenstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frankenstein) tells the story of Victor Frankenstein, a scientist who creates life and subsequently loses control over it. The narrative explores the dangers of unchecked ambition and the consequences of playing God. Shelley's work was a pioneering example of science fiction, tackling themes that remain relevant in AI discourse today.

**Why it matters:** Frankenstein's "creator plays God" trope has inspired numerous works of science fiction, including films like The Terminator and Ex Machina. This narrative device continues to influence how we think about the ethics of creating and controlling artificial intelligence.
