# Frankenstein: The First AI Nightmare

Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus," published anonymously in London on January 1, 1818, is a seminal work that introduced the world to the concept of artificial life gone awry.

**What happened:** In 1818, Mary Shelley, at the age of 20, published her Gothic novel "Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus." The story revolves around Victor Frankenstein, a young scientist who creates a sapient creature from various body parts, only to have it turn against him. Shelley began writing the novel in 1816 while traveling through Europe, inspired by the region's history and the scientific theories of the time, such as galvanism and alchemy. [Wikipedia — Frankenstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frankenstein)

**Why it matters:** "Frankenstein" is significant in the history of artificial intelligence because it presents the first modern narrative of artificial creation getting out of control. The novel established the trope of a creator playing God, a theme that continues to resonate in contemporary discussions about the ethics and risks of AI. Shelley's cautionary tale highlights the dangers of unchecked ambition and the responsibility that comes with technological advancement.
