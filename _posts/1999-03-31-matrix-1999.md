**The Matrix brought AI existential risk to mass culture and introduced simulation theory to mainstream audiences.**

**What happened:** The Wachowski sisters' groundbreaking film The Matrix (1999) revolutionized the sci-fi genre by depicting a dystopian future where humanity is unknowingly trapped inside a simulated reality created by intelligent machines. Lilly Wachowski, Lana Wachowski, and Keanu Reeves star in the film, which explores themes of free will, rebellion, and the blurred lines between reality and illusion. [The Matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrix) [Candide (Open Library)](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL100672W)

**Why it matters:** The Matrix's influence extended beyond entertainment, shaping public perception of AI and existential risk. Its red pill/blue pill metaphor inspired philosophical debates about the nature of reality, while Nick Bostrom's Simulation Argument (2003) built upon this idea. The film's impact can be seen in contemporary discussions around AI safety and the ethics of simulated realities.
