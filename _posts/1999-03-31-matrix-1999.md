# The Matrix: Simulated Reality and Machine Dominion

The Matrix, released in 1999, introduced the concept of simulated reality to a mass audience, sparking discussions about the nature of reality and the potential dangers of advanced AI.

**What happened:** In 1999, the Wachowskis (Lilly and Lana) directed *The Matrix*, a science fiction film that stars Keanu Reeves, Laurence Fishburne, and Carrie-Anne Moss. The movie presents a dystopian future where humans are unknowingly trapped in a simulated reality created by intelligent machines. Reeves plays Neo, a hacker who learns the truth about the Matrix and joins a rebellion against the machines.

**Why it matters:** *The Matrix* brought AI existential risk and simulation theory to the forefront of popular culture. Its iconic red pill/blue pill metaphor became a cultural shorthand for choosing between ignorance and truth. The film's influence was significant, as it preceded Nick Bostrom's Simulation Argument in 2003, which further explored the philosophical implications of simulated realities.
