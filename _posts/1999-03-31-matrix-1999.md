**The Matrix brought AI existential risk to mainstream culture in 1999**

**What happened:** The Wachowski siblings' groundbreaking sci-fi film introduced the concept of simulated reality and machine dominance, raising questions about the nature of existence and our relationship with technology. [The Matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrix) explored the idea that our reality might be a simulation created by intelligent machines. This narrative thread was later echoed in Nick Bostrom's Simulation Argument (2003). The film's iconic red pill/blue pill metaphor also became a cultural shorthand for confronting uncomfortable truths.

**Why it matters:** The Matrix's influence on AI can be seen in the growing interest in existential risk and the potential for advanced technologies to surpass human intelligence. The film's exploration of simulated reality has inspired discussions about the ethics of artificial intelligence and the need for more nuanced considerations of its development. Its impact can also be seen in the rise of philosophical debates surrounding the nature of consciousness and the limits of artificial intelligence.
