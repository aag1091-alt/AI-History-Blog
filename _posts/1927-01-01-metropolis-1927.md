The 1927 film Metropolis brought to life a robot that would shape AI's visual identity and themes.

**What happened:** Maria, the first AI robot on screen, was designed by Thea von Harbou and directed by Fritz Lang. In the film, Maria becomes a symbol of oppression, influencing countless humanoid robots and androids in science fiction [Metropolis (1927 film) — Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolis_(1927_film)]. Her design has been directly referenced in films like Star Wars' C-3PO and The Terminator.

**Why it matters:** Maria's portrayal of AI as a tool of oppression persists, reflecting concerns about the ethics of artificial intelligence. This theme has enabled discussions about AI's role in society and raises important questions about responsibility and control. As a result, Metropolis remains a significant milestone in AI history.
