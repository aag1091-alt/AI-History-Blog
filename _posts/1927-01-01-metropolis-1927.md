# Metropolis — The First AI Robot on Screen
In 1927, Fritz Lang and Thea von Harbou brought to life the first AI robot on screen in the groundbreaking film "Metropolis."

**What happened:** In 1927, Fritz Lang directed and Thea von Harbou co-wrote and co-produced the silent film "Metropolis," which introduced the world to the first humanoid robot in cinema. The robot, played by Brigitte Helm, was named Maria and served as a pivotal character in the film's dystopian narrative. Lang's vision of a futuristic city underscored the film's exploration of class struggle and the dangers of unchecked technological advancement.

**Why it matters:** The robot Maria in "Metropolis" set a template for the portrayal of humanoid robots and androids in science fiction. Its design and narrative impact have influenced countless subsequent works, including characters like C-3PO from "Star Wars" and the Terminator series. Moreover, the film's depiction of AI as a tool of oppression and control continues to resonate in contemporary discussions about the ethics and risks of artificial intelligence.
