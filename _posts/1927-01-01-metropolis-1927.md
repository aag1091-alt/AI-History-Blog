**A Futuristic Icon Was Born in a German Silent Film**

In 1927, Fritz Lang's science-fiction silent film Metropolis revolutionized the genre by introducing the first humanoid robot on screen. The robot Maria, with her expressionist design, became a template for countless humanoid robots and androids in science fiction, influencing works like C-3PO and the Terminator.

**What happened:** Metropolis was released in 1927, directed by Fritz Lang and written by Thea von Harbou, who also drew inspiration from Charles Dickens' Oliver Twist. The film's production was a groundbreaking endeavor, taking over 17 months to complete at a cost of millions of Reichsmarks [Metropolis (1927 film) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolis_(1927_film)]. Filming took place in the Babelsberg Studio for Universum Film A.G. (UFA).

**Why it matters:** Metropolis framed AI as a tool of oppression, a theme that persists in science fiction today. The film's influence can be seen in the depiction of humanoid robots and androids in various forms of media.
