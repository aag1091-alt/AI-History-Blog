# DeepSeek R1: China's Open Reasoning Model Shakes Silicon Valley
On January 20, 2025, DeepSeek, a Chinese AI company, launched DeepSeek-R1, a large language model that rivaled the capabilities of its American counterparts despite being developed under stricter chip export controls and with fewer, cheaper GPUs. The model was developed by the DeepSeek research team, led by Liang Wenfeng, the co-founder of the Chinese hedge fund High-Flyer.

**What happened:** Founded in July 2023 by Liang Wenfeng, DeepSeek released DeepSeek-R1 in January 2025. The model, which was trained for a reported $6 million, matched the performance of other leading large language models like OpenAI's GPT-4. [1]

**Why it matters:** DeepSeek-R1's launch was a significant moment in the AI industry, often referred to as an 'AI Sputnik moment' by US commentators. It demonstrated that AI progress was not solely dependent on access to the most powerful hardware, challenging the narrative that Silicon Valley had a technological moat. This breakthrough accelerated the geopolitical AI race and underscored the importance of innovative approaches to model development and deployment.
