# Llama: Open-Source AI Changes Everything

In 2023, Meta AI released Llama, a family of large language models that would soon become a cornerstone in the open-source AI movement. **What happened:** On February 24, 2023, Meta AI released Llama, a family of large language models that would soon become a cornerstone in the open-source AI movement. Initially, the models were only available to researchers under a non-commercial license, but unauthorized copies quickly spread via BitTorrent. Subsequently, Meta AI released more accessible versions, including Llama 2, which included instruction fine-tuned versions alongside foundation models. **Why it matters:** Llama's release was significant because it democratized access to powerful AI models, proving that smaller, open-source models could rival the capabilities of proprietary giants. This shift sparked the creation of numerous open-source alternatives like Alpaca, Vicuna, WizardLM, and Mistral. Yann LeCun, Meta's Chief AI Scientist, emphasized the importance of open-source AI in democratizing and distributing power in AI. Guillaume Lample, a researcher at Meta AI, also contributed to the development of Llama, highlighting the collaborative nature of the project. **Further reading:**
