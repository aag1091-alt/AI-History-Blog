# The Dartmouth Conference: AI Gets Its Name

The Dartmouth Conference in 1956 marked the birth of artificial intelligence as a scientific discipline.

**What happened:** In the summer of 1956, a group of mathematicians and scientists, including John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and Nathaniel Rochester, gathered at Dartmouth College for a six to eight-week brainstorming session. This workshop, known as the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence, was the first formal meeting dedicated to the study of artificial intelligence. McCarthy coined the term 'Artificial Intelligence' during this event, which would go on to define the field for decades to come.

**Why it matters:** The Dartmouth Conference is considered the founding event of AI, setting the stage for the development of the field over the next three decades. The attendees, who became known as the founding fathers of AI, laid the groundwork for future research and technological advancements in the area of machine learning and intelligent systems. Their conjecture that every aspect of learning and intelligence can be simulated by a machine has proven to be a cornerstone of AI research.
