**The Birth of Artificial Intelligence**
 
At the Dartmouth Conference on July 1, 1956, a group of pioneers coined the term 'Artificial Intelligence' that would shape the future of machine learning and beyond.

**What happened:** The conference brought together visionaries like John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and Nathaniel Rochester to explore the possibilities of creating machines that could simulate human intelligence. They proposed a research agenda centered on simulating all aspects of human intelligence, including learning. [Dartmouth Workshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_workshop)

**Why it matters:** The Dartmouth Conference marked a turning point in AI history, enabling the development of more sophisticated machine learning algorithms and inspiring generations of researchers to pursue this field. It laid the groundwork for the study of natural language processing, expert systems, and robotics. The conference's focus on simulating human intelligence has continued to shape the AI landscape.
