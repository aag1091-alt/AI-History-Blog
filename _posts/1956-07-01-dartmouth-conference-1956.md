**The Birth of Artificial Intelligence**

In 1956, a group of visionary researchers gathered at Dartmouth College to discuss the possibilities and challenges of creating intelligent machines.

**What happened:** [Dartmouth Workshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_workshop) was held in July 1956, marking a pivotal moment in AI history. The four-day workshop brought together experts like Claude Shannon, John McCarthy, Nathaniel Rochester, and Marvin Minsky to brainstorm ideas on creating artificial intelligence. Their discussions laid the groundwork for the field, which would go on to define the first 30 years of research.

**Why it matters:** The Dartmouth Conference is often referred to as "the Constitutional Convention of AI," as it established the foundation for the field. John McCarthy's coinage of the term "Artificial Intelligence" marked a turning point in popularizing the concept, and the attendees went on to shape the direction of AI research.
