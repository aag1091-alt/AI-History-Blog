On February 15, 2024, OpenAI unveiled Sora, a groundbreaking text-to-video model that transformed the way AI generates cinematic content. This development was closely followed by discussions from industry leaders such as Tim Brooks, Bill Peebles, and Sam Altman, who weighed in on the implications of Sora for the future of media and entertainment.

**What happened:** In February 2024, OpenAI previewed Sora, a text-to-video model capable of generating short video clips based on user prompts. The model was designed to extend existing short videos and integrate social media features. Sora's first generation was released to ChatGPT Plus and Pro users in the US and Canada in December 2024, with the second generation, Sora 2, reaching select users by September 2025. Despite the inclusion of a visible digital watermark to prevent misuse, third-party tools quickly emerged to remove this protection.

**Why it matters:** Sora marked a significant leap in AI's ability to create complex, detailed video content. The model's capability to generate scenes with multiple characters, specific motions, and accurate details challenged the film and media industries. Hollywood studios, directors, and visual effects artists immediately engaged in debates about the implications of AI-generated films. The shift from questioning whether AI could create images to whether it could produce full-length films underscored the profound impact of Sora on the creative landscape.
