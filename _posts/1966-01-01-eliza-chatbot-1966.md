In 1966, Joseph Weizenbaum developed ELIZA, a groundbreaking program that simulated human conversation, inadvertently sparking the chatbot phenomenon. **What happened:** ELIZA, created to explore human-machine interaction, used pattern matching to mimic a Rogerian psychotherapist, leading users to believe the machine understood them. This illusion, known as the 'ELIZA effect,' disturbed Weizenbaum, who later became a critic of AI's potential to mislead humans. **Why it matters:** ELIZA's impact lies in its demonstration of how humans can attribute understanding to machines, a phenomenon that continues to influence AI ethics and design today. **Further reading:** ["ELIZA Reinterpreted: The world's first chatbot was not intended as a chatbot at all"](http://arxiv.org/abs/2406.17650v2) by Jeff Shrager.
