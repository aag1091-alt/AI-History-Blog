# GPT-2: Too Dangerous to Release
In February 2019, OpenAI sparked a debate about AI safety and transparency with the staged release of GPT-2, a powerful language model capable of generating human-like text.

**What happened:** On February 14, 2019, OpenAI announced the creation of GPT-2, a large language model with 1.5 billion parameters, trained on 8 million web pages. The model could generate coherent text, including fake news articles, raising concerns about its potential misuse. OpenAI decided to stage the release, gradually making the model available to researchers, citing risks of misuse. Key figures behind GPT-2 include Alec Radford, Ilya Sutskever, and Dario Amodei.

**Why it matters:** The staged release of GPT-2 set a precedent for discussions around the ethical release of AI models. It highlighted the dual-use nature of AI technology, where the same capabilities that benefit society can also be harmful if misused. This episode underscored the need for responsible disclosure and the ongoing debate about who should control access to powerful AI systems.
