Opening sentence capturing why this moment matters.

**What happened:** In February 2019, OpenAI announced the creation of GPT-2, a large-scale language model capable of generating coherent text that could mimic human writing. The model's potential to produce convincing fake news and misinformation led OpenAI to stage its release, gradually making the model available to researchers while keeping it out of the hands of the general public. [GPT-2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-2) was a significant step in the development of AI language models, but it also raised concerns about the dual-use nature of such technology.

**Why it matters:** The GPT-2 release marked a pivotal moment in the debate over the responsible development and deployment of AI. It highlighted the tension between the benefits of open-source research and the risks associated with the misuse of powerful AI technologies. The episode set a precedent for future discussions on AI ethics, dual-use risks, and the role of organizations like OpenAI in managing the release of potentially dangerous technologies. [Alec Radford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alec_Radford), [Ilya Sutskever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Sutskever), and [Dario Amodei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dario_Amodei) were key figures in this decision, representing the broader concerns within the AI community about the implications of their work.
