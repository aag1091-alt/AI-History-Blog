In 2013, a groundbreaking technique called Word2Vec emerged, teaching machines to understand the nuanced relationships between words in a way that had never been seen before.

**What happened:** In 2013, a team at Google, including Tomáš Mikolov, Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, Ilya Sutskever, and Jeff Dean, introduced Word2Vec, a method for generating vector representations of words that capture their semantic meaning based on their context in large text corpora. This technique revolutionized natural language processing (NLP) by enabling machines to grasp the relationships between words, such as the famous analogy "King - Man + Woman ≈ Queen," which demonstrated the model's ability to capture complex semantic relationships.

**Why it matters:** Word2Vec laid the foundation for modern NLP, paving the way for advancements like BERT and GPT. By showing that machines could understand and represent the intricate relationships between words, Word2Vec opened up new possibilities for language understanding and generation, significantly advancing the field of AI.
