# Word2Vec: Teaching Machines the Meaning of Words
In 2013, a groundbreaking technique called Word2Vec emerged, revolutionizing how machines understand language by capturing the semantic relationships between words.

**What happened:** In 2013, a team at Google, including Tomáš Mikolov, Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, Ilya Sutskever, and Jeff Dean, introduced Word2Vec, a method for generating vector representations of words based on their context in large text corpora. This technique allowed machines to grasp the nuanced meanings of words and their relationships to one another. A notable demonstration of Word2Vec's capabilities was the equation 'King - Man + Woman ≈ Queen,' which illustrated the model's ability to capture semantic relationships.

**Why it matters:** Word2Vec's introduction marked a pivotal moment in the field of natural language processing (NLP). By enabling machines to understand the context and meaning of words, it laid the groundwork for subsequent advancements like BERT and GPT, which have transformed the way we interact with language models today.
