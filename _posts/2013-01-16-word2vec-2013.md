**The Birth of Word Embeddings Revolutionized AI**

In 2013, a breakthrough paper published by Tomáš Mikolov and his team at Google marked a significant turning point in the history of artificial intelligence. The paper introduced the Word2vec algorithm, a technique that enabled machines to capture semantic relationships between words, effectively creating vector representations of language.

**What happened:** [Word2vec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word2vec) was developed by Tomáš Mikolov, Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, Ilya Sutskever and Jeff Dean at Google, and published in 2013. The algorithm estimates word embeddings by modeling text in a large corpus, allowing for tasks such as detecting synonymous words or suggesting additional words for partial sentences.

**Why it matters:** Word2vec laid the foundation for all modern NLP models like BERT, GPT, and language models that followed, enabling AI to capture semantic relationships between words. This breakthrough facilitated the development of more sophisticated language processing capabilities, expanding the potential applications of AI in various fields.
