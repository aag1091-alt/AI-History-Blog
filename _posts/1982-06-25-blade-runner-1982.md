# Blade Runner: What Does It Mean to Be Human?

Blade Runner, released in 1982, remains a pivotal work in the exploration of artificial intelligence and humanity's place in a technologically advanced world.

**What happened:** Directed by Ridley Scott, Blade Runner is an adaptation of Philip K. Dick's 1968 novel *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?* The film is set in a dystopian future Los Angeles of 2019, where bioengineered beings called replicants, created by the Tyrell Corporation, are used for dangerous and labor-intensive work off-world. When a group of advanced replicants escape to Earth, former cop Rick Deckard (Harrison Ford) is tasked with hunting them down. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blade_Runner)

**Why it matters:** Blade Runner introduced the concept of replicants—synthetic humans nearly indistinguishable from their human counterparts. The film's exploration of memory, identity, and empathy as markers of consciousness has become a cultural touchstone for every subsequent debate about machine sentience. Its enduring influence is evident in the ongoing discussions about the nature of consciousness and the ethical implications of advanced AI.
