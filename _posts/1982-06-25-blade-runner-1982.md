**The Birth of a Cultural Touchstone**

Blade Runner, directed by Ridley Scott in 1982, revolutionized the sci-fi genre and introduced the concept of replicants – bioengineered beings indistinguishable from humans.

*What happened:* The film is an adaptation of Philip K. Dick's novel Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?, exploring memory, identity, and empathy as markers of consciousness. [Blade Runner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blade_Runner) follows a group of advanced replicants who escape back to Earth, leading former cop Rick Deckard on a perilous hunt.

**Why it matters:** Blade Runner's exploration of machine sentience and what it means to be human inspired the cultural debate about artificial intelligence. Its influence can be seen in many AI-related discussions, from ethics to consciousness. [Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? (Open Library)](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL2172356W) also laid the groundwork for Dick's exploration of these themes.
