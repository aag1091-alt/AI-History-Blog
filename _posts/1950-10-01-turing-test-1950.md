**The Turing Test Revolutionized AI Philosophy**

In 1950, Alan Turing proposed a test to measure a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to that of a human. [Turing Test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_test) 

The test involved a human evaluator judging a text transcript of a natural-language conversation between a human and a machine, trying to identify the machine. This marked a shift from philosophical debates about machine intelligence to empirical research in artificial intelligence.

The Turing Test redefined the question of machine intelligence, launching AI as a research discipline and remaining a cultural touchstone for AI consciousness debates 75 years later. [Computing Machinery and Intelligence (paper)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computing_Machinery_and_Intelligence)
