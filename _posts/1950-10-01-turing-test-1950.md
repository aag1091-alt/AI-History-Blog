*The moment AI began to confront its own existence.*

**What happened:** On October 1st, 1950, Alan Turing published his seminal paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing a test to determine whether machines could think. This paper introduced the concept of machine intelligence and sparked a philosophical debate that would shape the field of artificial intelligence. The Turing Test posits that a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human is sufficient to qualify as "thinking." [Computing Machinery and Intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computing_Machinery_and_Intelligence) [Turing Test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_test)

**Why it matters:** The Turing Test redefined the question of machine intelligence, launching AI as a research discipline. It remains the cultural touchstone for AI consciousness debates 75 years later, influencing the development of natural language processing, cognitive computing, and other areas of AI research.
