# The Turing Test: Can Machines Think?
In 1950, Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test, a groundbreaking method to assess machine intelligence, sparking a pivotal era in AI research.

**What happened:** In 1950, Alan Turing, a British mathematician and computer scientist, published his seminal paper 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence' in which he introduced the concept of the Turing Test. This test, originally called the imitation game, challenges a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human. Turing argued that if a machine could fool a human evaluator into believing it was human during a text-based conversation, then the machine could be said to think. [Turing Test — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_test)

**Why it matters:** The Turing Test redefined the question of machine intelligence from a philosophical debate to an empirical one, marking the birth of AI as a scientific discipline. Turing's proposal not only set the stage for future research in AI but also became a cultural touchstone for discussions about machine consciousness and the nature of intelligence. The test continues to influence AI research and public perception of AI capabilities 75 years later.
