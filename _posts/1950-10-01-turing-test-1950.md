# The Turing Test: Can Machines Think?

In 1950, Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test, a groundbreaking concept that redefined the question of machine intelligence from a philosophical debate to an empirical one.

**What happened:** In his seminal 1950 paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," Alan Turing introduced the idea of the Turing Test, originally called the imitation game. The test evaluates a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human. Turing argued that if a machine could fool a human evaluator into believing it was human through text-based conversation, then the machine could be considered to have demonstrated intelligence. [Turing Test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_test)

**Why it matters:** The Turing Test marked a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence. It shifted the discourse from abstract philosophical questions to practical, testable criteria, thereby launching AI as a serious research discipline. The test remains a cultural touchstone for discussions about machine consciousness and intelligence, even 75 years later. Turing's insight that the true measure of machine intelligence lies in its ability to mimic human thought processes has profoundly influenced the field of AI.
