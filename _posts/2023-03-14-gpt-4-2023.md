# GPT-4: Multimodal Reasoning at Human Expert Level
On March 14, 2023, OpenAI unveiled GPT-4, a groundbreaking multimodal large language model that redefined the capabilities of AI in reasoning and understanding complex tasks. This release was a significant milestone for OpenAI, led by key figures such as Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, and Ilya Sutskever, who have been instrumental in the development of AI technologies since the early 2010s.

**What happened:** GPT-4, developed by OpenAI researchers including Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, and Ilya Sutskever, was released in March 2023. This model not only surpassed previous versions in text generation but also demonstrated advanced multimodal reasoning, processing both text and images. Early versions of GPT-4 were integrated into Microsoft's Bing Chat and OpenAI's ChatGPT, showcasing its versatility and power. GPT-4's capabilities included passing the bar exam with a score in the 90th percentile and achieving top 10% results on the SAT, marking a significant milestone in AI's cognitive abilities. [1]

**Why it matters:** GPT-4's release shifted the conversation in the AI community from whether machines could reason to what limitations they might still have. This model's ability to understand and generate human-like responses across various domains, including legal and academic challenges, signaled a new era of AI that could potentially replace human experts in certain tasks. The model's multimodal capabilities also opened up new possibilities for AI in fields like visual recognition and interactive applications, pushing the boundaries of what AI can achieve. [2]
