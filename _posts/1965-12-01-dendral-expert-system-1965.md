# The First Expert System That Out-Performed Scientists (1965)

In 1965, the Dendral project at Stanford University produced the first expert system, marking a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1965, Edward Feigenbaum, Joshua Lederberg, Bruce Buchanan, and Carl Djerassi initiated the Dendral project at Stanford University. This pioneering effort aimed to automate the decision-making process of organic chemists by analyzing mass spectra data. The resulting software, Dendral, was the first expert system capable of outperforming human scientists in specific tasks. [Wikipedia — Dendral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dendral)

**Why it matters:** Dendral's success demonstrated that encoding domain expertise into rules could enable machines to solve complex problems that previously required extensive specialist training. This breakthrough launched the field of knowledge-based AI and directly inspired the expert systems boom of the 1980s, which attracted significant commercial investment.
