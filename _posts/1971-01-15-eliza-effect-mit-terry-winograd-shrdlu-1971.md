# SHRDLU: The Program That Understood a Block World (1971)

SHRDLU, a groundbreaking natural-language understanding program developed by Terry Winograd at MIT in 1971, revolutionized the field of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1971, Terry Winograd introduced SHRDLU, a program capable of understanding and responding to natural language commands within a simulated world of colored blocks. SHRDLU could move blocks, answer questions about their arrangement, and even engage in complex dialogues about the blocks' properties and relationships. The program was written in the Micro Planner and Lisp programming languages and ran on a DEC PDP-6 computer. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SHRDLU)

**Why it matters:** SHRDLU's ability to understand and interact with a simplified world of blocks was a significant milestone in AI, demonstrating the potential for computers to comprehend and respond to human language. However, its limitations outside this controlled environment highlighted the challenges of achieving true language understanding and inspired skepticism about purely computational approaches to AI.
