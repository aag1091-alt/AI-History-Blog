GPT-3's emergence marked a turning point in AI, sparking an 'AI is here' moment for the general public.

**What happened:** In May 2020, OpenAI released GPT-3, a large language model that showcased "zero-shot" and "few-shot" learning abilities. This milestone was achieved by a team led by Tom Brown, Benjamin Mann, Nick Ryder, Melanie Subbiah, Ilya Sutskever, and Sam Altman. The model's performance was so impressive that it triggered warnings from AI researchers, who described GPT-3 as "so good it's actually dangerous." [GPT-3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-3)

**Why it matters:** GPT-3's few-shot learning capabilities enabled it to perform tasks like writing code and prose with ease. This achievement demonstrated the potential of large language models and paved the way for future advancements in natural language processing. The AI Teacher Test, published on arXiv in 2022, further explored the pedagogical abilities of GPT-3 and Blender.
