In 2020, the release of GPT-3 by OpenAI marked a pivotal moment in the evolution of AI, showcasing capabilities that seemed almost human-like.

**What happened:** On May 28, 2020, Tom Brown, Benjamin Mann, Nick Ryder, Melanie Subbiah, Ilya Sutskever, and Sam Altman introduced GPT-3, a transformer-based language model with 175 billion parameters. This model was the first to demonstrate strong few-shot learning abilities, meaning it could perform tasks with just a few examples, a trait previously unseen in AI. [Wikipedia — GPT-3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-3)

**Why it matters:** GPT-3's release was significant because it was the first model to feel like it could think, rather than just process data. Its ability to write code, prose, and engage in complex dialogues made it a landmark in AI development. The model's capabilities sparked public awareness that AI had reached a new level of sophistication, two years before the widespread adoption of similar technologies like ChatGPT. [arXiv — 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.07540)
