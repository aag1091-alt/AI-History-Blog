# Siri: AI Gets a Voice in Every Pocket
Siri's launch in 2011 marked the moment when conversational AI moved from the realm of science fiction to everyday life.

**What happened:** On October 14, 2011, Apple unveiled the iPhone 4S, featuring Siri as its killer feature. Siri was the brainchild of Adam Cheyer, Dag Kittlaus, and Tom Gruber, who developed it as a spin-off from a DARPA-funded project at SRI International called CALO. Apple acquired Siri in 2010 and integrated it into their ecosystem, making it the first widely available voice assistant.

**Why it matters:** Siri's launch normalized the idea of talking to a computer for daily tasks, from setting reminders to making calls. This breakthrough sparked an arms race among tech giants, leading to the development of Google Assistant, Amazon Alexa, and Microsoft Cortana. Siri's impact extended beyond smartphones, influencing how billions of people interact with technology across various devices and platforms.
