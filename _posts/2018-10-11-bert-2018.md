**A Breakthrough in Language Understanding That Changed AI Forever**

In October 2018, Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, and Kristina Toutanova introduced a groundbreaking language model called BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers), which transformed the field of natural language processing (NLP). [BERT (language model)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BERT_(language_model))

**What happened:** BERT was trained using self-supervised learning and masked token prediction, allowing it to learn contextual representations of tokens. It improved upon previous models like ELMo and GPT-2, and its impact was felt across various NLP tasks, including coreference resolution and polysemy resolution.

**Why it matters:** BERT's bidirectional understanding of language enabled the creation of more accurate search engines, such as those used by Google, which powers billions of people daily. It also laid the foundation for "BERTology," a field that aims to interpret what is learned by BERT. The study of emoji prediction in tweets using BERT further showcased the model's versatility. [Emoji Prediction in Tweets using BERT](http://arxiv.org/abs/2307.02054v3)
