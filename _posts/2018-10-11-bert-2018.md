On October 11, 2018, Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, and Kristina Toutanova introduced BERT, a groundbreaking language model that revolutionized natural language processing.

**What happened:** In October 2018, researchers at Google published BERT, a transformer-based language model that uses bidirectional training to understand context more effectively. BERT is trained using masked token prediction and next sentence prediction, allowing it to learn nuanced representations of words within their context. This innovation marked a significant leap in the field of NLP.

**Why it matters:** BERT's introduction in 2018 dramatically improved the state of the art for language models, making it a ubiquitous baseline for NLP tasks. Its bidirectional training approach, which considers context from both directions, provided a fundamental insight into how machines can better understand language. BERT's impact extends beyond research, powering Google Search and enhancing the way billions of people interact with information daily.
