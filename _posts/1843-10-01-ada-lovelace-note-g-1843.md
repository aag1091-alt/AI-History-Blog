In 1843, Ada Lovelace published what is now considered the first computer program, a set of instructions for Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine.

**What happened:** In 1843, Ada Lovelace wrote "Note G," an extensive set of notes on Charles Babbage's proposed Analytical Engine. Her notes included a method for calculating Bernoulli numbers, which is widely recognized as the first published algorithm intended for implementation on a computer. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ada_Lovelace#Note_G_and_the_Bernoulli_numbers)

**Why it matters:** Lovelace's work was groundbreaking not only for its technical content but also for her prescient insights into the potential of computing machines. She foresaw the possibility of machines going beyond mere calculation to process symbols and concepts, a vision that laid the philosophical groundwork for modern computing. Her famous "Objection" to the idea that machines could originate anything was a precursor to debates in artificial intelligence that continue to this day.
