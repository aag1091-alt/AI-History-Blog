# The Neural Network That Shook Computer Vision (2012)

In 2012, Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton's AlexNet neural network dramatically improved image classification accuracy, marking a pivotal moment in artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 2012, Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton developed AlexNet, a deep convolutional neural network that achieved unprecedented accuracy in the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge. This model, with 60 million parameters and 650,000 neurons, significantly reduced the image classification error rate from 26% to 15%, showcasing the power of deep learning on GPUs. [AlexNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlexNet)

**Why it matters:** AlexNet's success proved that deep neural networks could surpass traditional hand-engineered feature methods, sparking a surge in AI research and investment. This milestone effectively ended the dominance of handcrafted algorithms in computer vision and reshaped the entire field of artificial intelligence.
