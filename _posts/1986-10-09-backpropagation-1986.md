Backpropagation, introduced in 1986, revolutionized the training of neural networks, paving the way for today's AI breakthroughs.

**What happened:** In 1986, David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams published a paper in Nature describing backpropagation, an algorithm that efficiently computes gradients for training multi-layer neural networks. This method allowed neural networks to learn complex patterns from data, overcoming a significant hurdle in AI research. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/323533a0)

**Why it matters:** Backpropagation solved the fundamental problem of training deep neural networks, which are the backbone of modern AI systems like ChatGPT and DALL-E. Without backpropagation, the development of these systems would have been impossible. Hinton's contributions were so impactful that he was awarded the Turing Award in 2018 for his work on neural networks.

Hinton's work on backpropagation was a pivotal moment in the history of AI, enabling the current era of machine learning and deep learning.
