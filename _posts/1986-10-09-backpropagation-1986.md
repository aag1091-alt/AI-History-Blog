**Solving the Neural Network Training Puzzle**

In 1986, three pioneers in AI research – David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams – cracked the code to training multi-layer neural networks with their groundbreaking algorithm: backpropagation.

**What happened:** Backpropagation revolutionized the way neural networks learn by allowing them to adjust their weights based on errors. [Backpropagation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation) enabled this process, paving the way for sophisticated AI systems like ChatGPT and DALL-E. The research was published in Nature (1986), where Rumelhart et al. introduced the technique: [Learning representations by back-propagating errors (Nature, 1986)](https://www.nature.com/articles/323533a0).

**Why it matters:** Backpropagation's impact cannot be overstated – it freed AI researchers from the limitations of earlier training methods, enabling the development of more complex and accurate neural networks. This breakthrough directly inspired many subsequent AI innovations.
