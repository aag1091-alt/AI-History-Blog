# Backpropagation: Teaching Neural Networks to Learn

Backpropagation, introduced in 1986, revolutionized the training of neural networks, paving the way for today's AI breakthroughs.

**What happened:** In 1986, David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams published a paper in Nature describing backpropagation, a method for training multi-layer neural networks. This algorithm efficiently calculates the gradient of a loss function with respect to the weights of the network, enabling faster and more effective training. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/323533a0)

**Why it matters:** Before backpropagation, training neural networks was computationally infeasible. This breakthrough made it possible to train deep networks, which are the backbone of modern AI systems like ChatGPT and DALL-E.
