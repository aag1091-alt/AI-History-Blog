Backpropagation revolutionized AI training in 1986, solving a fundamental problem that had been holding back neural networks for decades.

**What happened:** David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams introduced a new learning procedure called backpropagation to networks of neurone-like units [Learning representations by back-propagating errors (Nature, 1986)](https://www.nature.com/articles/323533a0). This method efficiently computed the gradient of a loss function with respect to the weights of the network, enabling neural networks to learn complex patterns and features. The authors described their new procedure in Nature, which marked a significant breakthrough in AI research.

**Why it matters:** Backpropagation enabled the training of multi-layer neural networks, paving the way for modern AI applications like ChatGPT, DALL-E, and many others [Backpropagation — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation). This milestone also laid the foundation for more advanced algorithms like ZORB, which presents a derivative-free backpropagation algorithm for faster training.
