# The Learning Algorithm That Refused to Die (1986)

In 1986, a groundbreaking paper by David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams reignited interest in neural networks by demonstrating the effectiveness of backpropagation for training multi-layer networks.

**What happened:** In 1986, David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, and Ronald Williams published a seminal paper in Nature, titled "Learning representations by back-propagating errors," which showed that backpropagation could train multi-layer neural networks to learn internal representations. This work overturned the pessimism surrounding neural networks following Minsky and Papert's critique in the 1960s and laid the computational foundation for modern deep learning systems. [Backpropagation - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation)

**Why it matters:** The 1986 paper by Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams is crucial because it revived connectionism as a serious research program and paved the way for the development of deep learning models. Their work demonstrated that backpropagation could effectively train neural networks with multiple layers, enabling the creation of more sophisticated and accurate models. This computational breakthrough has had a lasting impact on the field of artificial intelligence, leading to significant advancements in areas such as computer vision, natural language processing, and speech recognition.
