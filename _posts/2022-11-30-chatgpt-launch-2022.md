# ChatGPT: The Day AI Went Mainstream
On November 30, 2022, OpenAI launched ChatGPT, an AI chatbot that would redefine the relationship between humans and machines, marking the beginning of the Generative AI Explosion era.

**What happened:** On November 30, 2022, OpenAI, led by Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, and Ilya Sutskever, released ChatGPT, a generative AI chatbot that uses advanced language models like GPT-3 to engage in human-like conversations. The tool quickly gained 100 million users in just two months, setting a new record for consumer software adoption. [Wikipedia — ChatGPT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT)

**Why it matters:** ChatGPT's rapid rise to prominence underscores its significance in the AI landscape. It not only demonstrated the potential of generative AI to transform various professional fields but also sparked public discourse on the future of creativity and knowledge work. The release of ChatGPT is often compared to the iPhone moment in technology, as it made the capabilities of AI undeniable to everyone, forcing institutions to develop AI strategies overnight. As Elon Musk noted, "ChatGPT is eerily good at this. We are not far from dangerously strong AI." This moment marked a turning point in AI's journey from a niche technology to a mainstream phenomenon.
