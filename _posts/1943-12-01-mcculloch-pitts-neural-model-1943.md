# The Paper That Gave Machines Neurons (1943)

In 1943, Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts published a groundbreaking paper that laid the mathematical groundwork for neural networks and artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1943, Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts published "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity" in The Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics. This seminal work proposed a model of the nervous system as a network of simple logical elements, now known as artificial neurons or McCulloch-Pitts neurons. These neurons process inputs through a weighted sum and fire an output signal based on a threshold function, demonstrating that such networks could perform all logical functions. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Logical_Calculus_of_the_Ideas_Immanent_in_Nervous_Activity)

**Why it matters:** This paper bridged neuroscience and computation, establishing the conceptual blueprint for neural networks and artificial intelligence. By showing that simple on/off neurons could perform any computation a Turing machine could, McCulloch and Pitts paved the way for deep learning and other AI advancements. Their work was foundational in cognitive science, computational neuroscience, and computer science, influencing the field of automata theory and cited by John von Neumann as a significant result.
