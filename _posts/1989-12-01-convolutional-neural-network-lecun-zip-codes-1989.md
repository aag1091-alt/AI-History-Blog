In 1989, Yann LeCun and his colleagues at Bell Labs made a groundbreaking discovery that would revolutionize the field of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1989, Yann LeCun, Bernhard Boser, John Denker, and Larry Jackel published a paper demonstrating that convolutional neural networks (CNNs) could effectively read handwritten digits directly from pixel images, a task previously thought to require hand-designed feature extractors. This work, conducted at Bell Labs, marked a significant milestone in the development of deep learning and computer vision. [Backpropagation Applied to Handwritten Zip Code Recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network)

**Why it matters:** This breakthrough proved that neural networks trained with backpropagation could solve complex pattern recognition tasks, paving the way for modern applications in fields such as facial recognition and medical imaging. The convolutional neural network architecture refined by LeCun and his team has since become the backbone of many deep learning systems, enabling significant advancements in image processing and analysis.

- [Original Paper in Neural Computation](http://yann.lecun.com/exdb/publis/pdf/lecun-89e.pdf)

The impact of this research continues to be felt today, with CNNs remaining a cornerstone of deep learning and AI.
