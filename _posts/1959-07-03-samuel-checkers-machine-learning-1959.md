In 1959, Arthur Samuel coined the term "machine learning" while developing a checkers program that could learn from its mistakes and improve its gameplay.

**What happened:** In 1959, Arthur Samuel, an American computer scientist, developed a checkers program that could learn from its mistakes and improve its gameplay, a concept he termed "machine learning." This program was one of the first to demonstrate the ability of computers to learn without being explicitly programmed. [Arthur Samuel - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Samuel_(computer_scientist))

**Why it matters:** Samuel's work laid the foundation for the field of artificial intelligence and machine learning. His definition of machine learning as the ability of computers to learn without being explicitly programmed has become a cornerstone of the discipline, influencing countless advancements in AI over the decades.
