In 1976, MYCIN, an early expert system developed at Stanford University, demonstrated the potential of AI to match or even surpass human expertise in diagnosing and treating severe bacterial infections. Developed by Edward Shortliffe under the guidance of Bruce Buchanan, MYCIN used a backward chaining approach to identify bacteria and recommend appropriate antibiotics, outperforming general practitioners in clinical trials. [MYCIN - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycin)

**Why it matters:** MYCIN's success in a narrow medical domain showed that AI could rival human experts, setting a precedent for future AI applications in healthcare. Despite ethical and legal hurdles preventing its clinical deployment, MYCIN's legacy continues to influence AI research and its integration into medical practice.
