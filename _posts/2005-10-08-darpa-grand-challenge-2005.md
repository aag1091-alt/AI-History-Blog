**The DARPA Grand Challenge launched self-driving cars into the wild**

In 2005, a team of engineers and scientists from Stanford University led by Sebastian Thrun competed in the second annual DARPA Grand Challenge, a $2 million prize competition for autonomous vehicles. The event took place on October 8, 2005, with a grueling 212 km off-road course that tested the limits of their robotic drivers. [DARPA Grand Challenge (2005)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DARPA_Grand_Challenge_(2005))

The significance of this challenge cannot be overstated. It directly seeded the modern autonomous vehicle industry, enabling companies like Waymo and Uber ATG to develop self-driving cars. Thrun's team, which included Red Whittaker, successfully completed the course using a combination of LIDAR, GPS, and machine learning. Their achievement marked a major milestone in AI research.
