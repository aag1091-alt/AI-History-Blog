**The Game-Changer That Revolutionized AI Memory**

In 1997, Sepp Hochreiter and Jürgen Schmidhuber introduced Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), a groundbreaking architecture that tackled the vanishing gradient problem in recurrent neural networks. [Long short-term memory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_short-term_memory)

This innovation enabled RNNs to learn patterns spanning thousands of time steps, marking a significant breakthrough in AI's ability to remember and process sequences.

LSTM's impact on AI cannot be overstated. It paved the way for advancements in speech recognition, machine translation, and text generation, cementing its role as a foundational architecture that inspired subsequent innovations like transformers.
