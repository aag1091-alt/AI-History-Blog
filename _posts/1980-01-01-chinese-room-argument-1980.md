**The Chinese Room Thought Experiment: A Philosophical Objection to Strong AI**

In 1980, philosopher John Searle's thought experiment, "Minds, Brains, and Programs," challenged the assumption that passing the Turing Test implies real intelligence, sparking a debate that remains relevant today.

**What happened:** [Chinese room](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_room) argued that a computer executing a program cannot have a mind or understanding, regardless of its human-like behavior. Searle's centerpiece was the "Chinese room" thought experiment, where a person mechanically manipulates Chinese symbols according to rules, producing correct outputs without understanding Chinese.

**Why it matters:** Searle's argument has inspired research into the limits of artificial intelligence and continues to influence discussions on strong AI, the Turing Test, and the nature of consciousness. It remains the most influential philosophical objection to strong AI.
