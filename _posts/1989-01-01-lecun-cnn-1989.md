**The Birth of Image Recognition: Yann LeCun's Groundbreaking CNN**

In 1989, Yann LeCun created a machine that could reliably perceive visual patterns, paving the way for modern image recognition systems.

**What happened:** [Convolutional neural network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network) was first developed by Yann LeCun in 1989. LeCun's CNN was a major breakthrough, allowing machines to process and make predictions from images. This innovation has since enabled applications such as self-driving cars, medical imaging AI, and even processing US checks [1]. LeNet processed 10% of all US checks by 2000.

**Why it matters:** LeCun's work on CNNs laid the foundation for modern deep learning-based approaches to computer vision and image processing. The development of dropout training for convolutional neural networks in 2015 further improved performance and stability [2].
