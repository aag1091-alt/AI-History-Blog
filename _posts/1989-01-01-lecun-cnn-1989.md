In 1989, Yann LeCun introduced convolutional neural networks (CNNs), revolutionizing how machines perceive and understand visual data.

**What happened:** In 1989, Yann LeCun developed the first convolutional neural network (CNN) called LeNet, which was capable of recognizing handwritten digits with high accuracy. This breakthrough marked the beginning of CNNs as a powerful tool in the field of computer vision and deep learning. [Convolutional neural network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network)

**Why it matters:** LeCun's CNN was the first time a machine could reliably perceive visual patterns, laying the groundwork for modern image recognition systems, self-driving cars, and medical imaging AI. By 2000, LeNet was processing 10% of all US checks, demonstrating the practical impact of CNNs in real-world applications. As Yann LeCun noted, "You can think of deep learning as a way of automatically discovering the features that work best for a particular task."
