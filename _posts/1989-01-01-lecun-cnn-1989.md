**A Machine That Could See: The Birth of Convolutional Neural Networks**

In 1989, Yann LeCun and his team introduced a revolutionary AI system that could reliably perceive visual patterns, marking a significant milestone in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** [Convolutional neural network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network) was first developed by LeCun, allowing machines to automatically discover features that work best for a particular task. The LeNet system processed 10% of all US checks by 2000 and laid the foundation for modern image recognition systems, self-driving car cameras, and medical imaging AI.

**Why it matters:** LeCun's CNN enabled the development of modern computer vision and inspired a new generation of AI researchers to explore the possibilities of deep learning. The technology has since become a cornerstone of many applications, from facial recognition to autonomous vehicles.
