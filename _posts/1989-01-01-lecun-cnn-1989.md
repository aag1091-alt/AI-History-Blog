# Convolutional Neural Networks: Teaching Machines to See
In 1989, Yann LeCun introduced convolutional neural networks (CNNs), revolutionizing how machines perceive and understand visual data.

**What happened:** In 1989, Yann LeCun developed the first convolutional neural network, LeNet, which was capable of recognizing handwritten digits with high accuracy. This breakthrough marked the beginning of CNNs, which are now the backbone of many image recognition systems. LeNet's success paved the way for more complex CNN architectures that would later enable machines to process and understand vast amounts of visual data.

**Why it matters:** LeCun's work laid the foundation for modern computer vision and deep learning. CNNs have since been applied to a wide range of applications, from self-driving cars to medical imaging. By automating the process of feature extraction, CNNs have made it possible for machines to reliably perceive and interpret visual patterns, fundamentally changing the landscape of artificial intelligence.
