# The AI Too Dangerous to Release (2019)

In 2019, OpenAI made headlines by withholding the full release of GPT-2, a groundbreaking language model, due to concerns over its potential misuse.

**What happened:** In February 2019, OpenAI announced the development of GPT-2, a large language model with 1.5 billion parameters, trained on 8 million web pages. The model demonstrated remarkable capabilities in generating human-like text, translating languages, and answering questions. However, due to fears that it could be used to create convincing disinformation, OpenAI initially released only a smaller version of the model, delaying the full release until November 2019. Key contributors to GPT-2 include Alec Radford, Jeff Wu, Ilya Sutskever, Dario Amodei, and Jack Clark. [GPT-2 - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-2)

**Why it matters:** This decision marked a pivotal moment in AI ethics and governance. It sparked a debate about the responsible development and release of powerful AI systems, influencing future practices in the field. The incident highlighted the dual-use nature of AI technologies and the need for proactive measures to mitigate risks associated with misuse.
