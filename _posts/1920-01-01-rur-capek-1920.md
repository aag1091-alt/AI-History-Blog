**The Birth of a New World Order**

In 1920, playwright Karel Čapek's sci-fi play R.U.R. revolutionized the way we think about artificial intelligence by introducing the term "robot" to the global vocabulary.

**What happened:** [R.U.R. — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R.U.R.) introduced the word 'robot' (from Czech robota, meaning forced labor) and explored a theme of artificial workers revolting. This concept prefigured virtually every AI uprising narrative in science fiction. Čapek's work also sparked interest in robotics and automation, laying the groundwork for modern AI research.

**Why it matters:** R.U.R.'s depiction of robots gaining sentience inspired countless works of science fiction, including films like Terminator and I, Robot. The play's exploration of artificial intelligence also influenced the development of robotic law and ethics. The concept of a robot uprising has since become a staple of sci-fi, reflecting our ongoing anxieties about AI.
