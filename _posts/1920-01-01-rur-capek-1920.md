# R.U.R. — The Word 'Robot' is Born
In 1920, Czech writer Karel Čapek introduced the world to the word 'robot' in his play 'R.U.R.,' a term that would soon become a cornerstone of science fiction and technology discourse.

**What happened:** In 1920, Karel Čapek wrote 'R.U.R.,' short for 'Rossumovi Univerzální Roboti' (Rossum's Universal Robots), a play that premiered in 1921. The term 'robot' in the play's title comes from the Czech word 'robota,' meaning forced labor. [Wikipedia — R.U.R.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R.U.R.)

**Why it matters:** 'R.U.R.' not only coined the term 'robot' but also explored themes of artificial workers rebelling against their human creators, a narrative that has since become a staple in discussions about artificial intelligence and robotics. The play's influence extends beyond literature, shaping public perception and discourse around the ethics and implications of creating intelligent machines.
