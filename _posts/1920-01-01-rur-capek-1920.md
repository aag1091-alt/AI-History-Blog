**The World is Born into an Era of Artificial Intelligence**

On January 1st, 1920, Karel Čapek introduced a concept that would forever change the course of human history and science fiction: the word "robot." In his Czech play R.U.R., Čapek created a world where artificial workers rose up against their human creators, sparking a theme that has become synonymous with AI. The play's title translates to Rossumovi Univerzální Roboti (Rossum's Universal Robots), a phrase that has been used as a subtitle in English versions.

**What happened:** R.U.R. premiered on January 2nd, 1921, at Hradec Králové and was soon translated into thirty languages, becoming influential in Europe and North America. [R.U.R. — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R.U.R.) [R.U.R. and The insect play (Open Library)](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL575625W)

**Why it matters:** R.U.R.'s theme of artificial workers revolting has inspired virtually every AI uprising narrative in science fiction, shaping the way we think about the intersection of technology and humanity. This milestone enabled the concept of robot rebellion to become a staple of science fiction, paving the way for future explorations of AI's impact on society.
