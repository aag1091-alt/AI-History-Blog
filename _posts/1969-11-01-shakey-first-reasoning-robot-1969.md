# The Robot That Could Reason (1969)

In 1969, Shakey the Robot was born at the Stanford Research Institute, marking a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1969, Charles Rosen, Nils Nilsson, Peter Hart, and Bertram Raphael developed Shakey the Robot at the Artificial Intelligence Center of Stanford Research Institute (now SRI International). Shakey was the first general-purpose mobile robot capable of reasoning about its actions, combining logical reasoning, computer vision, and physical navigation into a single system. [Wikipedia — Shakey the robot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shakey_the_robot)

**Why it matters:** Shakey's development was groundbreaking because it was the first robot to integrate logical reasoning and physical action, setting the stage for future advancements in robotics and AI. The project led to significant contributions such as the A* search algorithm and the Hough transform, which remain fundamental tools in computer science and robotics today.
