# GPT-1: Language Models Learn to Generalize

GPT-1, released in 2018, marked a pivotal shift in how language models are trained and fine-tuned, setting the stage for today's most advanced AI systems.

**What happened:** On June 11, 2018, OpenAI introduced GPT-1, a generative pre-trained transformer-based language model. Developed by Alec Radford, Karthik Narasimhan, Tim Salimans, and Ilya Sutskever, GPT-1 demonstrated the effectiveness of the 'pretraining + fine-tuning' approach, which relies on unsupervised learning from vast amounts of text data. This method contrasts with previous models that heavily relied on supervised learning from manually labeled datasets. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPT-1)

**Why it matters:** GPT-1's introduction proved that language models could learn to generalize from large, unlabeled datasets, significantly reducing the need for extensive human annotation. This breakthrough paved the way for subsequent models like GPT-2, GPT-3, and eventually ChatGPT, which have revolutionized natural language processing and AI research. The model's success underscored the potential of unsupervised learning in scaling up AI capabilities across various languages and tasks. [OpenAI GPT-2 paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.08236)
