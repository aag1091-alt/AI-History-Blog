# AlphaFold: Solving Biology's 50-Year-Old Protein Problem

AlphaFold's breakthrough in 2020 marked a monumental shift in the field of structural biology, solving a problem that had eluded scientists for half a century.

**What happened:** In November 2020, DeepMind's AlphaFold 2 placed first in the CASP14 competition, demonstrating unprecedented accuracy in predicting protein structures. Developed by John Jumper, Richard Evans, and others under the leadership of Demis Hassabis, AlphaFold utilized advanced deep learning techniques to predict protein structures with remarkable precision. This achievement was a significant leap forward from the initial AlphaFold 1 version in 2018.

**Why it matters:** AlphaFold's success in predicting protein structures with high accuracy has profound implications for biological research, drug discovery, and disease understanding. By accelerating the process of identifying protein structures, AlphaFold has the potential to revolutionize fields such as medicine and biotechnology, making it one of AI's most impactful contributions to date.
