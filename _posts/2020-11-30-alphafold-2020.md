**AlphaFold revolutionized AI by solving biology's 50-year-old protein problem in a single system.**

**What happened:** In 2020, AlphaFold, an artificial intelligence program developed by DeepMind, solved the protein folding problem – a challenge that had stumped biologists for 50 years. [AlphaFold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaFold) achieved this milestone with remarkable accuracy, particularly in predicting structures for difficult-to-predict proteins. Its success was confirmed in subsequent CASP competitions, including [CASP14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.18446v1). Key people behind the project include Demis Hassabis, John Jumper, and Richard Evans.

**Why it matters:** AlphaFold's breakthrough has enabled significant advancements in fields like drug discovery, vaccine development, and cancer research. It has accelerated our understanding of diseases and paved the way for more accurate predictions of biomolecular interactions. [Accurate structure prediction of biomolecular interactions with AlphaFold 3](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07487-w_reference.pdf) further solidified its impact.
