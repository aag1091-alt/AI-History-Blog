# The Woman Who Gave AI Eyes (2009)

In 2009, Fei-Fei Li, Jia Deng, Wei Dong, and Kai Li launched ImageNet, a vast visual database that revolutionized the field of computer vision.

**What happened:** In 2009, Fei-Fei Li, Jia Deng, Wei Dong, and Kai Li introduced ImageNet, a large-scale hierarchical image database that provided the critical mass of labeled images needed for deep learning models to excel in visual recognition tasks. This project, which has since amassed over 14 million images across more than 20,000 categories, laid the groundwork for the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC), an annual competition that has become a benchmark for advancing computer vision research. [ImageNet - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ImageNet)

**Why it matters:** ImageNet's creation marked a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence, particularly in the realm of computer vision. By providing a massive, annotated dataset, ImageNet enabled researchers to train deep neural networks effectively, leading to significant breakthroughs such as AlexNet. This dataset and its associated challenge have driven the field forward for over a decade, making it possible for AI systems to recognize and understand visual data with unprecedented accuracy and efficiency.
