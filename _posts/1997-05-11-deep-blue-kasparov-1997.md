# Deep Blue Defeats Garry Kasparov

On May 11, 1997, Deep Blue, a supercomputer designed by Feng-hsiung Hsu, defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov in a historic match, marking a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1997, Deep Blue, a customized IBM RS/6000 SP supercomputer, faced off against reigning world chess champion Garry Kasparov in a six-game match. Deep Blue was the result of years of development, initially under the name ChipTest at Carnegie Mellon University, and later at IBM where it was renamed Deep Blue. In the 1997 match, Deep Blue won two games, drew three, and defeated Kasparov, a feat that was unprecedented at the time. [1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Blue_(chess_computer))

**Why it matters:** The victory of Deep Blue over Kasparov was a significant milestone in AI research. It demonstrated that a machine could outperform a human in a complex strategic game, challenging the notion that chess required human-like intelligence. However, the win also sparked debates about the nature of intelligence and whether brute-force search algorithms could be considered a form of genuine intelligence.
