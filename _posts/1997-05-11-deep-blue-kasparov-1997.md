Deep Blue's victory over Garry Kasparov marked a seismic shift in AI research.

**What happened:** On May 11, 1997, Deep Blue, a chess computer developed by IBM, defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov in a six-game match. [Deep Blue (chess computer)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Blue_(chess_computer)). This achievement was not just notable for its technological prowess but also for its implications on the nature of intelligence and the limits of brute-force search.

**Why it matters:** Deep Blue's win shattered the notion that chess mastery required human-like intelligence, sparking debate about what constitutes 'real' intelligence. It also demonstrated the power of computational complexity in solving complex problems. This milestone paved the way for AI applications in various fields beyond games.
