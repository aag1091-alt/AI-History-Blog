On June 10, 2014, Ian Goodfellow and his colleagues introduced the generative adversarial network (GAN), a groundbreaking concept in machine learning that has since revolutionized the field of generative artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In June 2014, Ian Goodfellow, while working under Yoshua Bengio at the University of Montreal, conceived the idea of GANs during a bar argument. The concept was revolutionary: two neural networks, a generator and a discriminator, compete against each other to improve the generator's ability to create realistic data. Goodfellow coded the first GAN in one night, marking the beginning of a new era in AI. [Generative adversarial network — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_adversarial_network)

**Why it matters:** GANs have had a profound impact on the AI landscape, enabling the creation of deepfakes, AI-generated art, and synthetic data. They have also advanced the generation of photorealistic images and videos, pushing the boundaries of what machines can create. This innovation laid the groundwork for the generative AI revolution, making it possible for AI to generate data that is nearly indistinguishable from real data.
