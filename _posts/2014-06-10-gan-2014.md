On June 10, 2014, Ian Goodfellow and his colleagues introduced generative adversarial networks (GANs), a groundbreaking technique that revolutionized the field of generative artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In June 2014, Ian Goodfellow, a PhD student at the University of Montreal under Yoshua Bengio, conceived the idea of GANs during a bar argument. The concept was based on the idea of two neural networks competing against each other, one generating data and the other trying to distinguish between real and generated data. Goodfellow coded the first GAN in one night, marking the birth of a powerful new approach to machine learning. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_adversarial_network)

**Why it matters:** GANs have had a profound impact on the field of AI, enabling the creation of deepfakes, AI-generated art, and synthetic data. They have also been instrumental in generating photorealistic images and videos, pushing the boundaries of what AI can achieve in terms of data generation and manipulation. This innovation has paved the way for numerous applications in entertainment, healthcare, and beyond.
