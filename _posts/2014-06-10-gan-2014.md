**The birth of generative AI revolution**

In June 2014, a revolutionary concept was born that would forever change the landscape of artificial intelligence. Ian Goodfellow and his colleagues, including Yoshua Bengio, proposed the idea of pitting two neural networks against each other in a zero-sum game, marking the beginning of the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) era.

**What happened:** Ian Goodfellow and Yoshua Bengio developed the concept of GANs, which initially aimed to generate new data with the same statistics as the training set. This technique learned to create realistic synthetic data, including photographs, that could fool human observers into thinking they were real. [Generative adversarial network — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_adversarial_network)

**Why it matters:** GANs enabled the creation of deepfakes, AI art, and photorealistic video generation, which have become increasingly prevalent in popular culture. Their impact has inspired new applications in fields such as computer vision, natural language processing, and reinforcement learning.
