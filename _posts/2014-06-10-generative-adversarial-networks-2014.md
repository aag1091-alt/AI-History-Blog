# The Birth of Generative Adversarial Networks (2014)

In 2014, a groundbreaking idea in artificial intelligence was born over drinks at a Montreal bar, leading to the creation of generative adversarial networks (GANs).

**What happened:** In June 2014, Ian Goodfellow, then a PhD student at the University of Montreal, and his colleagues Yoshua Bengio, Jean Pouget-Abadie, and Mehdi Mirza, introduced the concept of GANs. This innovative framework involves two neural networks competing against each other: a generator that creates synthetic data and a discriminator that evaluates the authenticity of the data. The paper detailing this concept was published later that year. [Link to original paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661).

**Why it matters:** GANs have revolutionized the field of generative artificial intelligence, enabling machines to produce increasingly realistic synthetic data. This technology has been pivotal in areas such as deepfakes, AI-generated art, data augmentation, and drug discovery, fundamentally altering the capabilities of AI systems.
