**The Power of Data: ImageNet's Revolutionary Impact on AI**

ImageNet, a massive visual database, was first released in 2009, changing the landscape of artificial intelligence forever.

**What happened:** ImageNet was developed by Fei-Fei Li and Jia Deng to provide a standardized dataset for image recognition research. The project hand-annotated over 14 million images with 20,000 categories, enabling software programs to correctly classify objects and scenes. [ImageNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ImageNet)

**Why it matters:** ImageNet's impact on AI was profound, as it enabled the creation of deep learning algorithms that could accurately recognize images and objects. This breakthrough inspired subsequent research and applications, such as object detection and scene understanding. The annual ILSVRC challenge, launched in 2010, further solidified ImageNet's role in shaping the field of visual recognition.
