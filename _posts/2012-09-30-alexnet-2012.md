# AlexNet: Deep Learning's Big Bang
In 2012, AlexNet's triumph in the ImageNet competition marked the dawn of deep learning's dominance in artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 2012, Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton introduced AlexNet, a convolutional neural network that achieved unprecedented accuracy in the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge. The model's success was due to its depth and the use of graphics processing units (GPUs) for training, which allowed it to surpass previous methods that relied on hand-engineered features. [Wikipedia — AlexNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlexNet)

**Why it matters:** AlexNet's performance was so far ahead of the competition that it effectively ended the era of hand-crafted features in computer vision. This breakthrough demonstrated the power of deep learning and prompted a rapid shift in AI research towards neural networks. The three authors went on to lead major AI initiatives, including OpenAI, Google Brain, and DeepMind, cementing the importance of their work in the field of artificial intelligence.
