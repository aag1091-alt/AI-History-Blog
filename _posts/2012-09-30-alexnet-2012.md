**The Big Bang of Deep Learning**

In 2012, AlexNet made history by defeating its hand-engineered features in image classification, forever changing the landscape of computer vision and artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** [AlexNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlexNet) was a convolutional neural network developed by Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton. The team's model achieved prominence in the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC), classifying images into 1,000 distinct object categories [1]. The original paper demonstrated that depth was essential for high performance, which became feasible due to the utilization of graphics processing units (GPUs) during training.

**Why it matters:** AlexNet's victory marked a turning point in AI history, enabling deep convolutional networks to become widely adopted and paving the way for future advancements [1]. The model's success inspired numerous applications in medical image classification [2].
