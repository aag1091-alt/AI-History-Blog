In 2012, AlexNet's groundbreaking performance in the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge marked the beginning of the deep learning revolution in artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 2012, Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, and Geoffrey Hinton developed AlexNet, a convolutional neural network that achieved unprecedented accuracy in image classification tasks. Their model, which contained 60 million parameters and 650,000 neurons, was trained on GPUs and won the ImageNet challenge by a significant margin. [Wikipedia — AlexNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlexNet)

**Why it matters:** AlexNet's success demonstrated the superiority of deep learning over traditional hand-engineered features in computer vision tasks. This pivotal moment prompted a rapid shift in the AI community towards deep learning, leading to advancements in various fields such as natural language processing and robotics. The team's subsequent work at major AI labs further solidified the impact of their initial breakthrough.
