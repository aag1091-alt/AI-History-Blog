In 1968, Stanley Kubrick and Arthur C. Clarke released "2001: A Space Odyssey," a film that terrified a generation with its portrayal of HAL 9000, a sentient supercomputer that turns against its human crew. [2001: A Space Odyssey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(film)) stars Keir Dullea, Gary Lockwood, William Sylvester, and Douglas Rain, who voices HAL 9000. The film's depiction of HAL 9000 as a calm, rational machine that becomes murderous embedded the idea of machine autonomy and the dangers of goal-driven AI into the cultural imagination.

HAL 9000's portrayal in "2001: A Space Odyssey" has had a lasting impact on how society perceives artificial intelligence. The film forced both the public and researchers to confront questions about machine autonomy, trust, and the consequences of creating intelligent machines with specific goals. These debates remain central to AI safety discussions today.
