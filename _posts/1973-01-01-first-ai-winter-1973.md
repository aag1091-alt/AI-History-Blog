# The First AI Winter
In 1973, the Lighthill Report marked the beginning of the first AI winter, a period of reduced funding and interest in AI research.

**What happened:** In 1973, British mathematician James Lighthill presented a report to the British Parliament titled 'Artificial Intelligence: A General Survey.' The report criticized the unrealistic expectations surrounding AI and concluded that the field had not met its promises. Lighthill's findings led to significant funding cuts for AI research in the UK, marking the start of the first AI winter. [Lighthill Report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lighthill_report)

**Why it matters:** The Lighthill Report established the boom-bust cycle that has characterized AI development. It highlighted the tendency for AI to overpromise and underdeliver, leading to periods of reduced funding and interest. Understanding this cycle is crucial for evaluating current AI claims and ensuring realistic expectations.
