The AI winter of 1973 marked a turning point in artificial intelligence research, exposing the field's tendency towards overhype and subsequent funding cuts.

**What happened:** The Lighthill Report, published in 1973, served as a wake-up call for the AI community, highlighting the gap between promised benefits and actual progress. This first major reckoning established the boom-bust cycle that would define future AI development cycles. James Lighthill's candid assessment of the field's limitations was a stark contrast to the optimism of the time: "In no part of the field have the discoveries made so far produced the major impact that was then promised." [Lighthill Report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lighthill_report) [AI Winter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_winter)

**Why it matters:** The Lighthill Report's findings enabled a more nuanced understanding of AI's potential and limitations, paving the way for future researchers to approach the field with a critical and realistic perspective. This awareness has helped mitigate the risk of subsequent hype cycles.
