**The AI Winter That Set the Stage for Reality**

In 1973, James Lighthill's report delivered a crushing blow to the burgeoning artificial intelligence field, exposing the hype and overpromising that had fueled its early years.

**What happened:** The Lighthill Report marked the first major reckoning for AI hype, establishing the boom-bust cycle that has defined AI development ever since. [AI Winter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_winter) had already begun to take hold, with funding cuts and dwindling optimism setting in as the reality of AI's capabilities sunk in. The report's scathing assessment served as a wake-up call for the field.

**Why it matters:** Understanding this cycle is crucial for evaluating current AI claims and managing expectations. The Lighthill Report also set the tone for future AI research, emphasizing the need for rigorous testing and evidence-based development.
