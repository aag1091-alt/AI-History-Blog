# The First AI Winter

The first AI winter began in 1973, marking a significant downturn in AI research funding and interest after an initial period of optimism.

**What happened:** In 1973, British mathematician James Lighthill presented a report to the British government, known as the Lighthill Report, which criticized the progress and achievements of AI research. Lighthill concluded that AI had not lived up to its promises and that the field was not making the expected advancements. His statement, "In no part of the field have the discoveries made so far produced the major impact that was then promised," highlighted the disappointment felt by many in the AI community. This report led to a reduction in funding for AI research in the UK and influenced similar funding cuts in other countries.

**Why it matters:** The Lighthill Report marked the first major AI winter, a period of reduced funding and interest in AI research. It established a pattern of hype followed by disappointment and funding cuts, a cycle that has repeated throughout the history of AI. Understanding this cycle is crucial for evaluating current AI claims and setting realistic expectations for future developments.
