Marvin Minsky's 1986 book "The Society of Mind" introduced a groundbreaking theory that intelligence arises from the interaction of simple, unintelligent components.

In 1986, Marvin Minsky published "The Society of Mind," a book that constructs a model of human intelligence as a society of mindless agents interacting in complex ways. This theory offered a radical alternative to both symbolic AI and neural networks, arguing that intelligence emerges from the interaction of many simple components rather than a single, unified entity. [Wikipedia — Society of Mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Mind)

The Society of Mind has had a lasting impact on fields from cognitive science to multi-agent systems and has anticipated modern ensemble and modular approaches to AI architecture. It challenged the prevailing views of AI and cognitive science at the time, influencing subsequent research and development in AI systems.
