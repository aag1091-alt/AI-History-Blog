**The Nobel Prize in Physics Recognizes AI's Place in Science**

In a groundbreaking ceremony held on October 8th, 2024, Geoffrey Hinton and John Hopfield were awarded the Nobel Prize for their work on neural networks, solidifying artificial intelligence's place within the realm of physics. [2024 Nobel Prize in Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Nobel_Prize_in_Physics)

This milestone marks a significant shift in how scientists view AI, as it establishes a formal acknowledgment that AI research is a legitimate pursuit of scientific inquiry. The recognition also underscores the potential risks associated with advanced AI systems, echoing concerns raised by Hinton during his press conference: "I am genuinely worried about the threat of very powerful AI systems doing things that are bad for us." [Nobel Prize in Physics: then and now](http://arxiv.org/abs/1603.09271v1)

The Nobel Prize's recognition of AI's place within physics enables further research into its applications and inspires new areas of study, such as the development of more advanced neural networks.
