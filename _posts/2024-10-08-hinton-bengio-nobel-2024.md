# Nobel Prize for AI: Hinton and Hopfield Win Physics Prize
On October 8, 2024, the Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Geoffrey Hinton and John Hopfield, marking a pivotal moment in the recognition of AI research as a cornerstone of physics.

**What happened:** On October 8, 2024, the Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Geoffrey Hinton and John Hopfield for their groundbreaking work in neural networks and artificial intelligence. Hinton, who left Google in 2023 to focus on AI risks, used the occasion to issue a stark warning about the dangers of advanced AI systems. This recognition highlights the transformative impact of AI on scientific research and technological advancement.

**Why it matters:** The Nobel Prize for Hinton and Hopfield signifies the scientific establishment's formal acknowledgment of AI as a critical field within physics. This recognition underscores the importance of AI in driving scientific progress and the need for responsible development and regulation of AI technologies.
