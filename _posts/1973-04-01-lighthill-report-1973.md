In 1973, the British Science Research Council commissioned a report by mathematician Sir James Lighthill that would have profound implications for the field of artificial intelligence (AI).

**What happened:** In 1973, Sir James Lighthill, a renowned mathematician, compiled a report titled "Artificial Intelligence: A General Survey" for the UK Science Research Council. The report, often referred to as the Lighthill report, critically evaluated the progress of AI research and concluded that the field had failed to deliver on its promises. Lighthill's assessment was particularly damning, stating that "In no part of the field have the discoveries made so far produced the major impact that was then promised." This report led to a significant reduction in AI funding in the UK and contributed to a period known as the AI winter, where funding and interest in AI research declined globally. [Lighthill report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lighthill_report)

**Why it matters:** The Lighthill report marked a pivotal moment in the history of AI, setting a precedent for the cyclical nature of hype and disappointment that would characterize the field for decades. The report's influence extended beyond the UK, affecting AI research worldwide and leading to a period of reduced funding and diminished public and academic interest in AI. [AI winter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_winter)
