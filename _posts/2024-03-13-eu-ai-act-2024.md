**What happened:** On March 13, 2024, the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act) was officially adopted, marking a significant milestone in global AI governance. The Act was spearheaded by European Commissioners Thierry Breton and Dragos Tudorache, alongside the efforts of European Parliament member Brando Benifei, who played crucial roles in shaping the legislation. [Artificial Intelligence Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence_Act)

**Why it matters:** The EU AI Act establishes a comprehensive regulatory framework that categorizes AI systems into different risk levels, ranging from minimal to high risk. It prohibits certain harmful practices such as social scoring and real-time biometric surveillance, imposes strict rules on high-risk systems like hiring tools, and mandates transparency requirements for generative AI. This legislation sets a global standard for AI regulation, much like the GDPR did for data privacy, and positions the EU as a leader in trustworthy AI development. As Commissioner Breton noted, "The AI Act is not just a rulebook. It is a launchpad for EU startups and researchers to lead in trustworthy AI."
