# The EU AI Act: The World's First AI Law

The European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act, signed into law in 2024, marks a historic moment in AI governance, setting a global standard for regulating AI technologies.

**What happened:** On March 13, 2024, the European Union officially adopted the Artificial Intelligence Act, a landmark regulation that establishes a comprehensive legal framework for AI systems within the EU. Spearheaded by European Commissioners Thierry Breton, Dragos Tudorache, and Brando Benifei, the Act categorizes AI applications based on their risk levels, ranging from unacceptable to minimal, and imposes strict rules on high-risk systems. The regulation entered into force on August 1, 2024, with phased implementation over the next 6 to 36 months.

**Why it matters:** The EU AI Act is significant because it provides a detailed regulatory framework that addresses the ethical, legal, and societal implications of AI. By banning certain high-risk applications and mandating transparency and accountability, the Act aims to foster public trust in AI technologies. This legislation not only sets a precedent for other nations to follow but also positions the EU as a leader in the development of trustworthy AI, much like GDPR did for data privacy.
