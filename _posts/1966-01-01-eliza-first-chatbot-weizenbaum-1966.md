# The Therapist That Was Never Alive (1966)

In 1966, MIT researcher Joseph Weizenbaum developed ELIZA, an early natural language processing program designed to simulate conversation. Using pattern matching and substitution, ELIZA could mimic human dialogue, particularly in the guise of a Rogerian psychotherapist, leading to the famous DOCTOR script. [Weizenbaum, 1966](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/365153.365168) details how ELIZA's simplistic approach still managed to convince users they were interacting with an intelligent entity.

**Why it matters:** ELIZA's ability to create an illusion of understanding had profound implications. It demonstrated how easily people could anthropomorphize machines, a phenomenon now known as the ELIZA effect. This milestone remains crucial in discussions about modern chatbots and the ethics of human-computer interaction.
