The Perceptron revolutionized AI by introducing the first learning machine in 1957.

**What happened:** Frank Rosenblatt's Perceptron was a groundbreaking electronic computer that could perform simple tasks like classification and pattern recognition [Perceptron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptron). The Navy unveiled its capabilities, predicting an AI system capable of "walk, talk, see, write, reproduce itself and be conscious of its existence" [New York Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptron). This early achievement laid the foundation for modern neural networks.

**Why it matters:** Rosenblatt's Perceptron enabled the development of future learning machines and inspired generations of AI researchers. Its ideas paved the way for significant advancements in machine learning, natural language processing, and deep learning.
