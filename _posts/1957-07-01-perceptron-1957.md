# The Perceptron: The First Neural Network

The Perceptron, introduced in 1957, marked the beginning of neural networks and laid the groundwork for modern AI.

**What happened:** In 1957, Frank Rosenblatt developed the Perceptron, an algorithm for supervised learning of binary classifiers. This groundbreaking work was detailed in his 1958 paper titled ["The perceptron: a probabilistic model for information storage and organization in the brain."](https://doi.org/10.1037/H0042519) The Perceptron was hailed as the first learning machine capable of processing and storing information in a way that mimicked the human brain.

**Why it matters:** The Perceptron's introduction was significant because it established the fundamental concept of a neural network, which underpins contemporary AI systems like GPT-4. However, the initial excitement was tempered by Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert's critique in 1969, which highlighted the Perceptron's limitations and led to the first AI Winter. Despite this setback, the Perceptron's legacy endures as a cornerstone of AI research.
