# The Perceptron: The First Neural Network

The Perceptron, introduced in 1957, marked the beginning of neural network research and laid the groundwork for modern AI.

**What happened:** In 1957, Frank Rosenblatt developed the Perceptron, an algorithm designed to mimic the function of a biological neuron. This groundbreaking work was the first step towards creating a machine that could learn and adapt based on input data. [Wikipedia — Perceptron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptron)

**Why it matters:** The Perceptron was the first learning machine, establishing the core concept behind every modern neural network, including models like GPT-4. Its introduction sparked significant interest and investment in AI research. However, the subsequent critique by Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert in 1969 led to the first AI Winter, temporarily halting progress in the field. Despite this setback, the Perceptron's legacy remains foundational to contemporary AI.
