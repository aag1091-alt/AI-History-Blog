In 1843, Ada Lovelace published a set of notes on Charles Babbage's proposed Analytical Engine, a mechanical general-purpose computer.

**What happened:**
In 1843, Ada Lovelace published an extensive set of notes on Charles Babbage's proposed Analytical Engine. Her notes included what is now recognized as the first algorithm designed to be processed by a machine, making Lovelace the first computer programmer. Additionally, she articulated the 'Lovelace objection,' which posits that machines can only do what they are programmed to do and cannot originate anything on their own.

**Why it matters:**
Lovelace's insights into the limitations of machines have echoed through the centuries, influencing debates in artificial intelligence. Her prescient observations about the nature of computation and machine capabilities continue to shape our understanding of AI's potential and boundaries.
