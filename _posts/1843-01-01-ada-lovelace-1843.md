**The Birth of Algorithmic Thinking**

In 1843, Ada Lovelace made history by writing the first algorithm for Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine, a proposed mechanical computer.

**What happened:** Ada Lovelace recognized the potential of the Analytical Engine and created a method for calculating Bernoulli numbers using it. Her notes on the engine contain the first computer program, predating modern programming concepts [Ada Lovelace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ada_Lovelace). This work sparked debate about the machine's capabilities, with Lovelace arguing that it could only perform tasks instructed by humans, a notion still relevant in AI discussions today.

**Why it matters:** Lovelace's work laid the foundation for modern computer science and inspired later pioneers of AI. Her notes on the Analytical Engine demonstrated an understanding of the power and limitations of machines, influencing the development of artificial intelligence.
