# Ada Lovelace: The First Algorithm
In 1843, Ada Lovelace published her notes on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine, a proposed mechanical general-purpose computer. **What happened:** Lovelace's work included what is now considered the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine, making her the first computer programmer. Her insights also introduced the 'Lovelace objection,' which posits that machines can only do what they are programmed to do and cannot originate anything on their own. **Why it matters:** Lovelace's contributions not only advanced the field of computing but also set the stage for ongoing discussions about the capabilities and limitations of artificial intelligence. Her prescient observations continue to influence how we think about the relationship between humans and machines. **Further reading:** [Ada Lovelace — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ada_Lovelace)
