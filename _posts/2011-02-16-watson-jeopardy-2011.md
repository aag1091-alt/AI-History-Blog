**The Tipping Point of Natural Language Processing**

In 2011, IBM's Watson computer system competed against two Jeopardy! champions and emerged victorious, marking a major milestone in natural language processing.

**What happened:** [Watson (computer) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Watson_(computer)): Developed by David Ferrucci and a research team, Watson was initially built to answer questions on Jeopardy! and went on to compete against Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings. The AI system won the first-place prize of US$1 million in 2011. [Analysis of Watson's Strategies for Playing Jeopardy! (arXiv)](http://arxiv.org/abs/1402.0571v1): This analysis highlights the key advances needed for IBM Watson to play Jeopardy! at a championship level, including rapid-fire answers and high precision.

**Why it matters:** The success of Watson marked a significant moment in AI history, enabling natural language processing to become more accessible and inspiring subsequent developments like Siri and Alexa. Its impact can also be seen in the advancements made possible by the sale of IBM's Watson Health division to Merative in 2022.
