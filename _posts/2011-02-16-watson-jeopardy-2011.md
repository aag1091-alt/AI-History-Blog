# Watson Wins Jeopardy: Natural Language Arrives

On February 16, 2011, IBM's Watson computer system defeated human champions Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings on the popular quiz show Jeopardy!, marking a significant milestone in the development of natural language processing.

**What happened:** In 2011, IBM's Watson, a computer system capable of answering questions posed in natural language, competed on Jeopardy! against two of the show's most successful contestants, Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings. Developed by a team led by David Ferrucci, Watson won the first-place prize of US$1 million. This victory showcased the system's ability to understand and respond to complex, nuanced questions in real-time, a feat previously thought to be the exclusive domain of human intelligence.

**Why it matters:** Watson's triumph on Jeopardy! was a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence, demonstrating the potential of natural language processing and machine learning. It paved the way for the development of conversational AI systems like Siri and Alexa, and laid the groundwork for more sophisticated applications in healthcare and other industries. Ken Jennings' memorable quote, "I for one welcome our new computer overlords," encapsulated the public's fascination and apprehension towards the capabilities of AI.
