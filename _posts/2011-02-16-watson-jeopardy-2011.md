On February 16, 2011, IBM's Watson computer system defeated human champions Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings in a historic Jeopardy! match, marking a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** IBM's Watson, a computer system developed by a team led by David Ferrucci, competed against two of Jeopardy!'s greatest champions, Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings. Watson's victory, which earned it the first-place prize of US$1 million, was a significant milestone in the field of natural language processing. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_Watson)

**Why it matters:** Watson's triumph showcased the capability of machines to understand and respond to complex, nuanced human language, a skill previously thought to be the exclusive domain of humans. This event not only made AI front-page news but also paved the way for the development of conversational AI assistants like Siri and Alexa, and eventually, large language models like ChatGPT. Ken Jennings' memorable Final Jeopardy answer, "I for one welcome our new computer overlords," encapsulated the public's fascination and apprehension towards AI's growing capabilities.
