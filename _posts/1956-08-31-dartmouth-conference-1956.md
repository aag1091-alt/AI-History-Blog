# The Conference That Named a Field (1956)

In the summer of 1956, a group of pioneering mathematicians and scientists gathered at Dartmouth College to explore the concept of machine intelligence, an event that would go on to define the field of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In 1956, John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and Nathaniel Rochester organized the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence. This six to eight week workshop, attended by eleven mathematicians and scientists, marked the birth of AI as a formal academic discipline. McCarthy coined the term 'artificial intelligence' in the proposal, which gave a shared identity to researchers working on machine reasoning, learning, and language. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_workshop) [Original Proposal](http://www-formal.stanford.edu/jmc/history/dartmouth/dartmouth.html)

**Why it matters:** The Dartmouth workshop's optimistic vision and the term 'artificial intelligence' it coined shaped funding priorities and research agendas for decades. It brought together key figures like Allen Newell and Herbert A. Simon, setting the stage for the rapid development of AI technologies and theories that would follow.
