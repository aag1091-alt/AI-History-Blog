# AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol: AI Masters Intuition
On March 9, 2016, AlphaGo, an AI program developed by DeepMind, defeated world-renowned Go player Lee Sedol in a historic match, marking a pivotal moment in the evolution of artificial intelligence.

What happened: From March 9 to March 15, 2016, AlphaGo, created by DeepMind's Demis Hassabis and David Silver, played a five-game match against South Korean professional Go player Lee Sedol in Seoul, South Korea. AlphaGo won four games and lost only one, showcasing its superior strategic capabilities. The most notable moment was Move 37, a counterintuitive move by AlphaGo that stunned Lee Sedol and highlighted the AI's ability to think beyond human intuition. [Wikipedia — AlphaGo versus Lee Sedol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo_versus_Lee_Sedol)

Why it matters: The victory of AlphaGo over Lee Sedol was a significant milestone in AI research, demonstrating that machines could master games of extraordinary complexity and intuition. Go, with its vast number of possible moves, was long considered a challenge too complex for AI. AlphaGo's success proved that AI could surpass human understanding in strategic thinking, opening new avenues for AI development and application in fields requiring deep strategic insight.
