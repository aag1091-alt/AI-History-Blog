# AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol: AI Masters Intuition

On March 9, 2016, AlphaGo, an AI program developed by DeepMind, made history by defeating world champion Lee Sedol in the ancient game of Go, a feat once thought impossible for machines.

**What happened:** From March 9 to 15, 2016, AlphaGo, created by Demis Hassabis and David Silver, played a five-game match against Lee Sedol in Seoul, South Korea. AlphaGo won four games and lost only one, marking a significant milestone in AI. Notably, during the second game, AlphaGo made an unconventional move known as "Move 37," which stunned Lee Sedol and the AI community. [AlphaGo versus Lee Sedol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo_versus_Lee_Sedol)

**Why it matters:** Go, with its vast number of possible moves, was long considered too complex for AI to master. AlphaGo's victory demonstrated that AI could develop strategic intuition beyond human understanding, a critical step in the evolution of machine intelligence. Lee Sedol's acknowledgment of AlphaGo's creativity underscored the profound impact of this achievement. The match highlighted the potential of deep learning and neural networks to solve problems once thought unsolvable by machines.
