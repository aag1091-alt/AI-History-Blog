**Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics: A Foundation for AI Alignment**

Isaac Asimov's groundbreaking proposal in 1942 set the stage for modern AI ethics by introducing a framework to ensure machines prioritize human safety.

*   **What happened:** In his seminal work, "Runaround," Asimov published three laws that aimed to regulate robot behavior, including the prohibition of harming humans and taking their lives. The first law stated: "A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm." [Three Laws of Robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Laws_of_Robotics) These laws were designed to prevent robots from causing harm and have been widely cited by AI safety researchers, ethicists, and engineers.

*   **Why it matters:** Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics laid the groundwork for modern AI alignment efforts, highlighting the importance of prioritizing human well-being in robotic decision-making. Their inherent contradictions revealed the challenges of achieving true AI alignment, serving as a reminder that aligning AI systems with human values is an ongoing and complex task.
