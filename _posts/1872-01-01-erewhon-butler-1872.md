In 1872, Samuel Butler published "Erewhon," a novel that eerily foreshadowed modern debates about artificial intelligence and machine consciousness.

**What happened:** In "Erewhon," Butler imagined a society where machines are evolving and could potentially surpass humans in intelligence. This novel, set in a fictional country, is a satire of Victorian society and explores the philosophical implications of machine evolution. Butler's work was influenced by Charles Darwin's "On the Origin of Species" and the rapid technological advancements of the Industrial Revolution. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erewhon)

**Why it matters:** "Erewhon" is significant for being one of the first works to seriously consider the possibility of machine consciousness and the ethical implications of superintelligent machines. Butler's novel raises questions about the nature of life, consciousness, and the relationship between humans and technology that remain relevant today. His prescient observations about the potential dangers of advanced AI and the need for societal adaptation to technological change have influenced subsequent discussions on AI ethics and alignment. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erewhon)
