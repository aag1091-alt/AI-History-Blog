In 1872, Samuel Butler published "Erewhon," a novel that eerily foreshadowed modern debates about artificial intelligence and machine consciousness.

**What happened:** In "Erewhon," Butler imagined a society where machines are treated as living beings capable of evolving consciousness, a concept that was revolutionary for its time. The novel's exploration of machine intelligence was influenced by Charles Darwin's "On the Origin of Species" and the rapid technological advancements of the Industrial Revolution. Butler's work is a satire of Victorian society, but it also contains profound philosophical inquiries into the nature of machines and their potential to surpass human intelligence. [Wikipedia — Erewhon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erewhon)

**Why it matters:** "Erewhon" is significant because it was one of the first literary works to seriously consider the possibility of machine consciousness and the ethical implications of such a development. Butler's novel anticipated many of the concerns that modern AI researchers and ethicists grapple with today, such as the alignment problem and the potential for machines to evolve beyond human control. His prescient insights into the relationship between technology and society continue to resonate in contemporary discussions about AI.
