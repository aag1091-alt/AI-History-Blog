In 1872, Samuel Butler published "Erewhon," a novel that introduced the world to the idea of machines evolving consciousness, a concept that remains relevant in today's discussions about artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** In "Erewhon," Samuel Butler, drawing from his experiences as a sheep farmer in New Zealand, created a satirical utopian society where machines are feared and outlawed because they are seen as potential competitors to humans. The novel is notable for its early exploration of artificial intelligence, influenced by Charles Darwin's "On the Origin of Species" and the rapid industrialization of the late 19th century. Butler's work is one of the first to seriously consider the philosophical implications of machine consciousness and the potential for machines to evolve beyond human control. [Wikipedia — Erewhon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erewhon)

**Why it matters:** "Erewhon" is significant in the history of AI because it was one of the earliest works to raise ethical and philosophical questions about the nature of consciousness and the potential risks of superintelligent machines. Butler's prescient concerns about the alignment of AI with human values and the dangers of unchecked technological progress continue to resonate in contemporary debates about AI ethics and safety.
