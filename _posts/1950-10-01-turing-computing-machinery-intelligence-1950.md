In 1950, Alan Turing published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," a seminal paper that introduced the concept of the Turing test, a method for determining a machine's ability to demonstrate intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.

**What happened:** In 1950, Alan Turing published his influential paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" in the journal *Mind*. Turing proposed the Imitation Game, now known as the Turing test, which reframes the question "Can machines think?" into a practical, empirical test. [Read more](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computing_Machinery_and_Intelligence).

**Why it matters:** Turing's paper not only introduced the Turing test but also anticipated and addressed many of the major objections to artificial intelligence that would arise in the decades to follow. It remains one of the most cited works in the philosophy of artificial intelligence, fundamentally shaping the field's direction and discourse.
