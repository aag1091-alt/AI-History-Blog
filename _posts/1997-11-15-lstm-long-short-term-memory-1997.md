# The Network That Learned to Remember (1997)

In 1997, Sepp Hochreiter and Jürgen Schmidhuber introduced Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, a breakthrough in artificial neural networks. LSTM addressed the vanishing gradient problem in recurrent neural networks (RNNs), enabling them to learn long-term dependencies in sequential data. This innovation was published in the journal *Neural Computation* and later detailed on Wikipedia.

**Why it matters:** LSTM networks revolutionized the field of deep learning, powering applications like Google Translate, Apple's Siri, and Amazon's Alexa for nearly two decades. Their ability to remember information over long periods made them indispensable for tasks requiring understanding of context and sequence, such as speech recognition and machine translation.
