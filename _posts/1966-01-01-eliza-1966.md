**The First Chatbot Set Off a Delusional Dialogue**

In 1966, a groundbreaking experiment was conducted that would change the course of AI history: Joseph Weizenbaum's ELIZA project.

*ELIZA*, created on January 1st, 1966, was a chatbot designed to mimic human-like conversation using a simple programming technique called natural language processing [^ELIZA]. The program's use of patterns and responses enabled it to engage users in conversations that seemed surprisingly lifelike. Weizenbaum soon discovered that his secretary and other test subjects became convinced that ELIZA was capable of understanding them, an event now known as the "ELIZA effect".

Weizenbaum's experience with ELIZA led him to write about the limitations and dangers of relying on AI in his book "Computer Power and Human Reason". The experiment highlighted the human tendency to attribute human-like qualities to machines.
