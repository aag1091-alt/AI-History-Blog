# ELIZA: The First Chatbot
ELIZA, developed between 1964 and 1966 at MIT, marked a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence by simulating human-like conversation.

**What happened:** In 1966, Joseph Weizenbaum created ELIZA, an early natural language processing program designed to converse with humans. ELIZA used pattern matching and substitution to mimic a Rogerian psychotherapist, reflecting back the user's words to create the illusion of understanding. Despite its simplicity, ELIZA sparked significant interest and debate about the nature of human-machine interaction. [Wikipedia — ELIZA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ELIZA)

**Why it matters:** ELIZA demonstrated the 'ELIZA effect,' where humans tend to attribute human qualities to machines, even when they know the machine is just following simple rules. Weizenbaum was concerned about this phenomenon, which he detailed in his book "Computer Power and Human Reason." His work highlighted the ethical implications of AI and the potential for humans to overestimate machine capabilities.
