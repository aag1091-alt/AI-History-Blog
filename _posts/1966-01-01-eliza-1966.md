# ELIZA: The First Chatbot
ELIZA, developed in 1966 by Joseph Weizenbaum, marked a pivotal moment in the history of artificial intelligence.

**What happened:** From 1964 to 1967, Joseph Weizenbaum created ELIZA at MIT, a program designed to simulate conversation through pattern matching and substitution. ELIZA's most famous script, DOCTOR, mimicked a Rogerian psychotherapist, reflecting back users' words to them. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ELIZA)

**Why it matters:** ELIZA demonstrated the 'ELIZA effect,' where humans tend to attribute understanding and emotions to machines. Weizenbaum was surprised by how easily people were convinced that ELIZA understood them, leading him to write "Computer Power and Human Reason" to caution against over-reliance on AI. This highlighted the ethical and psychological implications of human-computer interaction.
