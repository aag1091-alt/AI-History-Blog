**ELIZA: The First Chatbot**

In 1966, Joseph Weizenbaum created ELIZA, a natural language processing program that simulated conversation using pattern matching and substitution methodology.

**What happened:** Developed at MIT from 1964 to 1967, ELIZA was designed to explore communication between humans and machines. Its most famous script, DOCTOR, simulated a psychotherapist and gave users an illusion of understanding through rules and responses dictated in the script [ELIZA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ELIZA). The program's fame was largely due to Weizenbaum's own intention to obscure its purpose.

**Why it matters:** ELIZA demonstrated the "ELIZA effect," where humans tend to anthropomorphize machines, leading to powerful delusional thinking. This phenomenon has had lasting impacts on AI research and development [ELIZA Reinterpreted: The world's first chatbot was not intended as a chatbot at all](http://arxiv.org/abs/2406.17650v2).
