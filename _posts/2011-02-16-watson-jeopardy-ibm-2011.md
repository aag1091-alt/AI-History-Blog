In 2011, IBM's Watson artificial intelligence system defeated human champions Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter in the game show Jeopardy!, marking a significant milestone in AI development.

**What happened:** In 2011, IBM's Watson AI system, led by project director David Ferrucci, competed on the popular American quiz show Jeopardy! against two of its most successful contestants, Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter. Watson's victory showcased its ability to process and understand complex human language, including puns and wordplay, at a superhuman speed. [Watson (artificial intelligence)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Watson_(artificial_intelligence)) [DeepQA Project at IBM Research](https://researcher.watson.ibm.com/researcher/view_group.php?id=2099)

**Why it matters:** This event was pivotal in shifting public perception of AI capabilities, demonstrating that machines could comprehend and respond to natural language with remarkable accuracy and speed. It marked a significant step forward for IBM, which pivoted its focus toward commercial AI services following Watson's success. The Jeopardy! victory remains one of the most watched and celebrated demonstrations of AI's potential.
