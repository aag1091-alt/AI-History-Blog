In 1966, MIT professor Joseph Weizenbaum developed ELIZA, a program that simulated conversation, leading many to believe it understood human emotions.

**What happened:** In 1966, MIT professor Joseph Weizenbaum created ELIZA, a computer program designed to simulate conversation using pattern matching and substitution methods. Despite not being intended as a chatbot, ELIZA's DOCTOR script, which mimicked a Rogerian psychotherapist, convinced many users that the program genuinely understood their emotional states. [Wikipedia — ELIZA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ELIZA)

**Why it matters:** The 'ELIZA effect' demonstrated how people can attribute understanding and empathy to machines that merely respond with pre-programmed patterns. This phenomenon remains crucial in discussions about AI ethics and the potential for human-computer interactions to deceive or manipulate users. [Shrager, J. (2024).
